Orientation
This note explains how overstretched, under-resourced, or structurally unstable public systems create conditions where individuals can escalate concerns opportunistically. Escalation may arise from bias, fear, personal conflict, or convenience rather than genuine risk. This is a systems-level vulnerability, not a theory of individual malice. The node helps describe why escalation spikes during periods of institutional stress.
