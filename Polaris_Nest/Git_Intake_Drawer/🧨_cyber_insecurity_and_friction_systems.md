## Cyber Insecurity Is Not Just a Technical Problem

Cyber incidents are often discussed in terms of:
- data breaches
- ransomware
- system downtime
- hostile state activity

But their most consequential effects are **operational and social**.

They:
- interrupt services
- degrade trust
- force fallback procedures
- and amplify existing inequalities

Cyber insecurity is a **governance stressor**, not just an IT risk.

## What Friction Systems Do Under Stress

Friction systems include:
- algorithmic triage
- risk scoring
- prioritisation dashboards
- automated queues
- access gating mechanisms

Under normal conditions, these systems already:
- ration attention
- delay some users
- accelerate others
- externalise harm as â€œedge casesâ€

Under cyber stress, these effects intensify.

## Failure Modes That Matter

When systems are attacked, degraded, or partially disabled:

- automated processes fail closed rather than open
- manual overrides are slow or unavailable
- staff rely more heavily on risk scores
- uncertainty is treated as danger
- contestability collapses

In these conditions, friction does not pause.  
It **hardens**.

## Who Absorbs the Harm

- cannot self-advocate
- lack alternative routes
- generate extensive data exhaust
- are already classified as â€œriskyâ€
- depend on timely state response

This includes:

- disabled people
- those subject to policing or probation

- favours centralisation over redundancy

- reduce perceived chaos
