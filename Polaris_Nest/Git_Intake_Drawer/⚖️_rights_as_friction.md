## Courts as System Interrupts

Courts and legal processes:
- slow decisions
- reopen settled cases
- question assumptions
- demand evidence
- prioritise process over outcome

From a delivery-focused perspective, this is â€œblockage.â€

From a rights perspective, it is **system correction**.

- legal uncertainty feels intolerable

- reframe rights as abuse

## The ECHR Problem (Structural, Not Symbolic)

The European Convention on Human Rights constrains:
- automated decision-making without safeguards
- disproportionate surveillance
- group-based suspicion
- weak routes to redress
- executive overreach in security contexts

In platform-mediated governance, these constraints:
- slow deployment
- complicate procurement
- introduce legal risk
- and limit scale

This is why hostility to rights frameworks often increases **after** infrastructure is embedded.

The system is already running.  
The law is now â€œin the way.â€

## Why Rights Fail First in Crisis

Under crisis conditions:
- urgency is moralised
- delay is framed as harm
- challenge is reframed as irresponsibility

Rights are recast as:
- luxuries
- technicalities
- activist obstruction
- or threats to security

This logic is especially powerful when:
- cyber insecurity is high
- geopolitical rhetoric escalates
- public fear is elevated

In such moments, rights do not disappear â€” they are **selectively suspended**.

## Friction Is a Feature, Not a Bug

Rights exist precisely because:
- systems make mistakes
- power concentrates
- harm is unevenly distributed
- efficiency benefits the powerful first

Friction:
- slows harm propagation
- exposes error
- creates space for challenge
- and protects minorities from optimisation logic

Removing friction does not make systems fairer.  
It makes them faster at doing harm.

---

## Who Pays the Cost When Friction Is Removed

When rights are treated as friction, the burden shifts to:
- migrants
- disabled people
- children in care
- welfare recipients
- over-policed communities
- political dissidents

These groups:
- cannot opt out
- cannot see the system
- cannot easily challenge outcomes

Efficiency is achieved by **externalising harm**.
