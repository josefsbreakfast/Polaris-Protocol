This node examines how, during the Gaza war period, **internal dissent pathways narrowed simultaneously** in both the UK and Israel.

## ðŸ”• Dissent as a system function
In healthy systems, dissent:

- provides early warning,
- slows escalation,
- surfaces blind spots,
- and enables course correction.

Dissent is not noise.  
It is a **safety mechanism**.

When dissent is suppressed, systems lose braking capacity.

## ðŸ‡¬ðŸ‡§ UK: narrowing without bans
In the UK, dissent was constrained through:

- concentration of authority in ministers,
- absence of lawful refusal routes,
- professional codes reframed as neutrality requirements,
- security clearance and confidentiality pressure,
- and reputational risk to careers.

Key features:
- no formal prohibition,
- but rising personal cost,
- and no protected escalation path.

Resignation becomes the only clean exit.

## ðŸ‡®ðŸ‡± Israel: narrowing through law
In Israel, dissent contraction was more explicit:

- emergency regulations expanded,
- speech restrictions tightened,
- protest policing intensified,
- whistleblowing chilled,
- and legal penalties for opposition increased.

Within the military:
- refusal framed as betrayal,
- discipline tightened,
- and internal critique securitised.

The result was **formal closure**, not just drift.

Both reduce correction capacity.
