## 3. The Formation of Proxy Embeddings

Proxy embeddings occur when a recommender system must resolve:

- conflicting behaviours  
- irregular rhythm  
- contradictory pacing  
- inconsistent preference vectors  

To stabilise its representation, the model collapses multiple shadows into a **single archetype**.

This often results in:

- recommendations aimed at older demographics  
- content aligned with another gender  
- relational patterns interpreted through a second signature  
- safety content aimed at dynamics the system infers statistically  
- hybridised preference profiles

The system is not detecting a second person.  
It is approximating one.

## 4. Why Machine-Learning Systems Misclassify the Shadow Actor

The model has no way to label the interfering pattern as:

- administrative,  
- procedural,  
- institutional,  
- bureaucratic, or  
- structural.

Instead, it maps the shadow to the **closest behavioural archetype**, usually:

> a dominant or controlling interpersonal figure.

This mapping is not personal.  
It is statistical default behaviour in high-ambiguity spaces.
