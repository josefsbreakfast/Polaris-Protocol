## 4) Why This Persists  

### Cost Asymmetry  
- **Doubt is expensive once, cheap forever.**  
  A single well-funded case can generate expert scripts, citations, and judicial language that are reused indefinitely at marginal cost.

### Moral Asymmetry  
- **Wrongful convictions are visible and narratively clean.**  
- **Missed abuse is diffuse, delayed, and often silent.**  
Public sympathy, media attention, and donor interest therefore skew toward the former.

### Risk Asymmetry  
- **Institutions are punished for false positives, not false negatives.**  
Regulators, prosecutors, and clinicians respond rationally by retreating, hardening procedure, and raising thresholds.

### Incentive Alignment Without Coordination  
No actor needs to share ideology or intent.  
Aligned incentives alone produce cumulative effects:
- litigation rewards uncertainty  
- research rewards caution  
- institutions reward risk avoidance  

The result is a stable equilibrium biased toward inaction.

## 5) Boundary Conditions (What This Is *Not*)  

This analysis does **not** claim that:
- child abuse is fabricated or rare  
- scepticism is illegitimate or malicious  
- all funders or advocates share ideology  
- disagreement equals denial  

It **does** document that:
- selective uncertainty can be structurally weaponised  
- edge cases can become baselines through repetition  
- evidentiary accretion can outpace protective capacity  

Legitimate scepticism becomes harmful **only when its downstream effects are ignored**.
