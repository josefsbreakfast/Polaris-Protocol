## I. Buyer Behaviours That Enable Recovery

### 1. Shift from browsing to following  
**Behaviour change:**

**Behaviour change:**

- one meaningful purchase beats five cheap ones

Scarcity here is not artificial.  
It is structural honesty.

## I. Asks for Regulators

### 1. Formally recognise micro-exporters

### 2. Treat clone-driven price dumping as competition harm  
Reclassify systematic design cloning combined with extreme undercutting as an **unfair competition issue**, not merely an IP dispute.

Clone economies exploit jurisdictional gaps and legal asymmetry.  
Competition law is the appropriate tool.

### 3. Mandate marketplace transparency  
Require large marketplaces to publish:
- percentage of listings by production type (handmade / artist-designed / mass manufacture)

### 4. Enforce â€œhandmadeâ€ as a consumer protection claim

## II. Asks for Platforms  
*(marketplaces, app stores, intermediary infrastructure)*

### 5. Enforce mandatory tier separation  
Platforms must clearly separate:
1. Handmade by maker  
2. Artist-designed, ethically manufactured  
3. Resale / mass manufacture

The problem is not tier 2 existing â€” it is tier 2 impersonating tier 1.

### 6. Introduce listing caps and scaling friction

- hard listing caps for â€œhandmadeâ€ sellers

### 7. Shift clone detection to the platform level

- remove the burden of endless takedown requests from individual creators  

Pattern detection is a platform capability, not a creator responsibility.

---

### 8. Guarantee portable identity and audience rights  
Creators must be able to:
- export customer contacts (with consent)  
- export reviews and reputation data  
- notify followers if they leave the platform  

Lock-in currently functions as coercive dependency.

## III. Asks for Legislators  
*(trade, tax, cultural policy)*

### 9. Establish VAT safe harbours for cultural micro-commerce  
Create simplified VAT treatment for low-value cultural exports (prints, books, decks, art objects), below defined turnover and shipment thresholds.

The current system assumes accountants and customs brokers.  
Cultural micro-commerce cannot bear that burden.

---

### 10. Introduce trade carve-outs for cultural goods  
Treat independent cultural exports more like books and small press, and less like bulk consumer imports.

The cost of friction here is **cultural erosion**, not merely lost revenue.

---

### 11. Expand IP toward provenance rights  
Legislate provenance-based protections, including:
- right to be credited as originator  
- penalties for systematic design laundering  
- pattern-based enforcement mechanisms  

IP law protects objects.  
Clone economies exploit patterns.

---

### 12. Fund non-platform cultural infrastructure  
Public funding should support:
- cooperative marketplaces  
- shared fulfilment hubs  
- local and regional distribution networks  
- open-source marketplace tooling  

If all routes to market run through extractive platforms, recovery is structurally impossible.
