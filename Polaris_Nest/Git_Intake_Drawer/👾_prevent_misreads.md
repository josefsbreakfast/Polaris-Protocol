## Orientation
This note covers the systematic misinterpretation of distress, trauma expression, neurodivergent traits, frustration, or anger within PREVENT-shaped environments. These misreads are not anomalies; they come from subjective criteria, system pressure, staff undertraining, and multi-agency anxiety. This node documents the pattern, not the intent, and shows how lived experience is pathologised as potential radicalisation.

## Why these misreads happen

## Trauma responses mistaken as warning signs
Trauma can present as:
- hypervigilance
- emotional flooding
- withdrawal
- sudden anger
- crying during appointments
- erratic sleep
- inconsistent communication
Staff without appropriate training may interpret these as instability, secrecy, or potential future harm. PREVENT framing encourages looking for patterns, even when the pattern is simply trauma.

## Cumulative impact on individuals
Repeated misreads lead to:

## Why this matters
Misreads erode the legitimacy of PREVENT and damage relationships between communities and institutions. They also conceal genuine risks by overwhelming the system with false positives derived from misinterpreted emotional or neurological traits.
