### 1. From sequence to structure  

Proteins start as a one-dimensional amino-acid sequence.  
Their function emerges only once they fold into a three-dimensional structure.

Data follows the same pattern:

- Logs, fields, timestamps, and flags are linear  
- Meaning emerges only through aggregation, inference, and use  
- The operational â€œshapeâ€ of data is not visible in the raw record  

What matters is not what the data *is*, but **how it has folded inside the system**.

---

### 2. Folding is not deterministic  

Protein folding does not proceed step-by-step toward a single obvious outcome.  
The chain explores multiple configurations simultaneously, guided by an energy landscape with ridges, traps, and local minima.

Data systems do the same:

- Dashboards, models, and workflows create interpretive landscapes  
- Some interpretations are cheap, fast, and institutionally comfortable  
- Others are costly, slow, or reputationally risky  

Data therefore â€œfalls into shapeâ€ â€” not because it is true, but because **the system makes that shape stable**.

---

### 3. Energy landscapes in data systems  

In biology, energy corresponds to physical stability.  
In data systems, the equivalent energies are:

- Administrative effort  
- Legal exposure  
- Reputational risk  
- Time and labour  
- Political cost  

Low-energy interpretations spread.  
High-energy corrections stall.

This is why misclassification persists even when everyone privately knows it is wrong.

---

### 4. Early folds dominate  

In protein physics, early folding decisions strongly constrain later possibilities.  
Some pathways close forever.

In data systems:

- Initial flags  
- Early categorizations  
- First interpretations by authority  

â€¦shape everything that follows.

Later reviews rarely re-open the folding process.  
They merely add annotations to an already-collapsed structure.

---

### 5. Misfolding and stabilisation  

Misfolded proteins do not simply fail.  
They often aggregate, propagate, and become toxic.

Misfolded data behaves similarly:

- Errors replicate across systems  
- Risk labels propagate via sharing agreements  
- Templates overwrite individual reality  
- Corrections fail to propagate  

The system treats the fold as identity.

---

### 6. Chaperones and their removal  

Biological systems rely on **chaperone proteins** to prevent misfolding and guide correct structure.  
They consume energy and do not appear in the final product.

In data systems, chaperones are:

- Caseworkers  
- Human review panels  
- Ethics committees  
- Appeals processes  
- Ombuds and oversight roles  

They are expensive.  
So systems design them out.

When chaperones disappear, misfolding becomes permanent.

---

### 7. Irreversibility without physics  

Protein misfolding is often irreversible because the pathway back is lost.

Data misfolding becomes irreversible because:

- Context disappears  
- Authority hardens  
- Decisions have already been acted upon  
- Institutions refuse to admit error  

The information may still exist, but **the route back does not**.

This is denaturation in data systems.
