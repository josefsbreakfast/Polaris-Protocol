## ğŸ§· Research threads in critique / limitations (what could be wrong)
### 1) Construct proliferation / diagnostic inflation
Risk: RACI becomes â€œa new label for old phenomenaâ€ (insomnia, hyperarousal, PTSD-related hypervigilance).  
Mitigation: keep RACI as **mechanism** + **formulation tool**, not necessarily a standalone disorder.

### 2) Causality and confounds
- Noise exposure, trauma history, disability stressors, and administrative uncertainty are highly confounded in real life.
- Many studies are observational; attribution requires careful design and/or within-person longitudinal measures.

### 3) Measurement problem (operationalising RACI)
RACI requires measurable markers beyond self-report:
- attentional capture metrics (e.g., vigilance tasks; attentional bias paradigms)
- autonomic markers (HRV patterns; nocturnal sympathetic surges)
- endocrine rhythm (diurnal cortisol slope; evening cortisol)
- sleep architecture (micro-arousals; fragmentation; REM/NREM disruption)

Without measurement consensus, RACI risks becoming rhetorically powerful but empirically mushy.

### 4) Individual differences
Baseline trauma exposure, neurodivergence, sleep disorders, medication effects, and working memory capacity can moderate susceptibilityâ€”potentially making RACI heterogeneous.

### 5) Risk of misinterpretation in adversarial contexts
In institutional or legal settings, â€œconditioningâ€ language may be misread as implying delusion or misattribution.

**Scope:** practical steps that reduce (a) conditioned orienting / hyperarousal, (b) circadian disruption, and (c) downstream neurometabolic load â€” without requiring attribution of intent.

### 1) Reduce unpredictability at the stimulus layer (highest leverage)
RACI is amplified by *unpredictable, uncontrollable salience*. Primary goal: convert â€œrandom hitsâ€ into â€œbounded signalsâ€.
- **Notification triage:** disable non-essential push notifications; batch delivery where possible; whitelist only user-set alarms/reminders.

- **Stimulus control:** keep phone out of bedroom; use a separate, single-purpose alarm clock if needed.

### 2) Noise exposure mitigation (sleep + autonomic stability)

- **Night-time noise reduction:** physical dampening (draft excluders, curtains, door seals), relocation of chargers/transformers away from bed, trial sleeping in a different room if feasible.
- **Document variability:** note timing/patterns of recurrent noise *only insofar as it supports mitigation* (avoid spiralling into exhaustive monitoring).
- **Caution with masking noise:** constant masking can help some people sleep, but if it increases vigilance or annoyance, stop.

- **Stimulus control** (bed = sleep; leave bed if wide awake; return when sleepy).
- **Sleep restriction therapy** (tightening time-in-bed to rebuild sleep drive).
- **Relapse planning** (because re-triggering is expected in RACI trajectories).

### 4) Circadian re-entrainment (light + timing)

### 5) Autonomic downshift training (physiology-first)
Because micro-arousals and autonomic shifts can occur without full waking, downshifting sympathetic tone is often helpful.
- daily paced breathing / HRV biofeedback style practice (short, regular doses)
- gentle daytime movement + daylight exposure (supports sleep pressure + circadian anchoring)

### 6) Neurometabolic monitoring (if symptoms persist)
