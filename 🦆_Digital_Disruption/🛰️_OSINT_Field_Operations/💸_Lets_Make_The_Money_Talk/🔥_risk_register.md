## Risk: Speculation Drift

**Description:**  
Analysis slides from institutional patterns into conjecture about individuals, intent, or guilt.

**Impact:**  
- legal exposure,  
- survivor harm,  
- loss of credibility.

**Mitigation:**  
- enforce institutional focus,  
- require sources for all claims,  
- pause work when inference replaces evidence.

---

## Risk: Survivor Re-Extraction

**Description:**  
Survivors are repeatedly asked to comment, clarify, or legitimise work.

**Impact:**  
- retraumatisation,  
- withdrawal,  
- ethical breach.

**Mitigation:**  
- default to non-engagement,  
- treat survivor silence as binding,  
- shift accountability labour to institutions.

---

## Risk: Escalation Through Heat

**Description:**  
Public attention, anger, or provocation accelerates decisions.

**Impact:**  
- boundary collapse,  
- unsafe disclosure,  
- reputational damage to the work.

**Mitigation:**  
- apply heat management protocols,  
- slow publication,  
- rotate responsibility.

---

## Risk: Nudging and Behavioural Capture

**Description:**  
Participants are subtly redirected toward distraction, urgency, or futility.

**Impact:**  
- loss of agency,  
- fragmentation,  
- burnout.

**Mitigation:**  
- return to procedural anchors,  
- resist urgency framing,  
- document pressure effects.

---

## Risk: Platform and Algorithmic Distortion

**Description:**  
Search, recommendation, or moderation systems distort visibility.

**Impact:**  
- misinformation amplification,  
- loss of signal,  
- uneven reach.

**Mitigation:**  
- rely on primary documents,  
- maintain off-platform archives,  
- avoid platform-specific optimisation.

---

## Risk: Legal Overreach

**Description:**  
Participants exceed lawful boundaries through enthusiasm or misunderstanding.

**Impact:**  
- personal risk,  
- invalidation of findings,  
- chilling effect.

**Mitigation:**  
- reinforce lawful OSINT limits,  
- avoid private data,  
- seek review when unsure.

---

## Risk: Capture by Bad-Faith Actors

**Description:**  
Hostile or opportunistic actors attempt to steer scope or tone.

**Impact:**  
- agenda distortion,  
- internal conflict,  
- reputational harm.

**Mitigation:**  
- governance and moderation protocols,  
- selective engagement,  
- refusal of adversarial debate.

---

## Risk: Antisemitic or Racialised Framing

**Description:**  
Inquiry collapses into culturally primed scapegoating.

**Impact:**  
- harm to communities,  
- analytical failure,  
- coalition fracture.

**Mitigation:**  
- apply open-minded institutional analysis,  
- resist convenience narratives,  
- foreground domestic accountability.

---

## Risk: Burnout and Attrition

**Description:**  
Sustained exposure to harm narratives exhausts participants.

**Impact:**  
- loss of continuity,  
- degraded judgement,  
- withdrawal.

**Mitigation:**  
- division of labour,  
- exit permission,  
- pacing and rest.

---

## Risk: Loss of Legitimacy

**Description:**  
The work is framed as activist, conspiratorial, or vengeful.

**Impact:**  
- dismissal by regulators,  
- public mistrust,  
- reduced effectiveness.

**Mitigation:**  
- procedural tone,  
- transparent method,  
- refusal of spectacle.
