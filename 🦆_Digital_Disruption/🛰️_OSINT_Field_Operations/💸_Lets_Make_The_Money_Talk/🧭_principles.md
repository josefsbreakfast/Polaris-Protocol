## Survivor Sovereignty

Survivors are not evidence repositories.  
They are not public property.  
They are not obligated to disclose, explain, educate, or relive harm.

Survivor sovereignty means:
- survivors decide whether, when, and how they engage;
- survivor silence is respected without interpretation;
- survivor withdrawal is final and requires no justification;
- survivor stated wishes override public curiosity.

Listening includes stopping.

This work exists to **shift the burden of accountability away from survivors and back onto institutions and power**.

---

## Structural Analysis Over Personal Accusation

This project analyses:
- systems,
- institutions,
- financial and governance structures,
- incentive environments,
- and patterns of benefit.

It does not:
- accuse private individuals,
- speculate about guilt,
- crowdsource theories,
- or substitute inference for evidence.

The operative question is **who benefits**, not who to blame.

---

## Lawful Process as Leverage

All work must operate strictly within:
- the law,
- established transparency mechanisms,
- and formal accountability channels.

This includes:
- public-record OSINT only;
- FOI / SAR used precisely and proportionately;
- regulator escalation based on documented non-compliance.

Unlawful access, harassment, intimidation, or coercion are prohibited.

Process is not a delay tactic here.  
Process is the point of leverage.

---

## Harm Reduction, Not Catharsis

This project does not exist to satisfy outrage.

It exists to:
- reduce the capacity for further harm;
- raise the cost of impunity;
- destabilise abusive infrastructures;
- and strengthen weakened legal and civic norms.

Actions are evaluated by **downstream harm reduction**, not emotional release.

---

## Discipline Over Speed

Speed benefits evasion.  
Discipline benefits accountability.

Work should be:
- slow enough to be correct;
- boring enough to be defensible;
- repetitive enough to accumulate pressure.

Virality is not a goal.  
Persistence is.

---

## Non-Violence and Refusal of Threat

Violence, threats, or rhetoric endorsing physical harm are incompatible with this work.

They:
- endanger survivors,
- delegitimise accountability,
- justify repression,
- and fracture coalitions.

Justice pursued through fear is not justice.  
This project refuses it entirely.

---

## Transparency as a Democratic Right

Transparency laws are not privileges granted by the state.  
They are democratic rights exercised by the public.

Using them:
- is not hostile,
- is not subversive,
- and is not anti-patriotic.

Attempts to weaken transparency in response to lawful scrutiny are themselves governance signals and must be documented, not feared.

---

## Division of Labour

No individual is expected to do everything.

This work assumes distributed contribution:
- legal expertise,
- financial literacy,
- data analysis,
- humanities and social analysis,
- documentation and archiving.

Coordination replaces heroism.  
Structure replaces chaos.

---

## AI as Tool, Not Authority

AI systems may be used for:
- drafting,
- summarisation,
- administrative support,
- and formatting.

AI systems must not be used to:
- interpret survivor testimony,
- generate moral judgement,
- speculate about intent,
- or launder euphemism.

Human judgement retains authority at all times.

---

## Termination Conditions

Any activity must stop immediately if it:
- endangers survivors,
- invites harassment or vigilantism,
- exceeds lawful boundaries,
- or compromises these principles.

Sustainability and legitimacy outweigh momentum.
