## Broadcast as Civic Infrastructure

Accountability does not operate only through courts and regulators.

It also relies on:
- public recall,
- shared reference points,
- and cultural persistence.

Broadcast provides the background signal that prevents quiet abandonment.

---

## Beyond News Cycles

News attention is episodic.
Accountability is cumulative.

When coverage drops:
- institutions often wait,
- processes stall,
- and consequences diffuse.

Broadcast bridges the gap between moments of attention.

---

## Forms of Broadcast

Broadcast is not limited to formal media.

It includes:
- updates and summaries,
- visual artefacts and infographics,
- slogans and repeated phrases,
- art, music, and design,
- humour and meme culture,
- badges, stickers, and merchandise,
- and community-led expression.

Different forms reach different people.
All contribute to presence.

---

## Normalisation Without Trivialisation

Broadcast keeps issues visible without requiring constant intensity.

Repetition:
- reduces novelty,
- resists shock fatigue,
- and embeds concern as normal rather than exceptional.

Normalisation sustains engagement.
It does not minimise harm.

---

## Cultural Signal Versus Instruction

Broadcast should:
- signal shared values,
- mark collective refusal,
- and express solidarity.

It should not:
- instruct investigation,
- direct action,
- or personalise blame.

Signal is safer than command.

---

## Memes as Memory Anchors

Memes:
- compress ideas,
- travel easily,
- and persist across platforms.

Used responsibly, they:
- counter euphemism,
- puncture reputational fog,
- and remind without overwhelming.

Humour can coexist with seriousness.
Cruelty cannot.

---

## Merchandise as Low-Stakes Participation

Merchandise offers:
- non-verbal support,
- low-risk engagement,
- and durable presence.

It allows people to:
- care without explaining,
- signal alignment without debate,
- and contribute without exposure.

This is not commodification.
It is distribution of concern.

- maintain continuity,
- prevent drift,

## Avoiding Broadcast Capture

Broadcast should avoid:
- naming private individuals,
- escalating claims,
- or rewarding outrage.

Clarity, restraint, and repetition outperform shock.
