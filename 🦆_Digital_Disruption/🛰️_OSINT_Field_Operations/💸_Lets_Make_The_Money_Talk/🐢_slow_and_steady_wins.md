- legal teams,
- narrative infrastructure,
- and resource asymmetry.

- rewards reactive framing,

- persisting longer than attention cycles,
- accumulating records over time,

- repeated FOI compliance failures,
- recurring reputational spend anomalies,
- consistent procedural gaps.

## Survivors and Temporal Respect

Survivor timelines do not align with public urgency.

Slow work:
- reduces retraumatisation,
- avoids repeated exposure,
- and allows survivors to disengage safely.

Justice pursued at survivor pace is more likely to endure.

## Boredom as Protection

Work that is:
- procedural,
- repetitive,
- and unglamorous

is harder to capture, attack, or sensationalise.

Boredom protects legitimacy.

---

## Attrition Versus Burnout

Slow pacing:
- preserves capacity,
- distributes labour,
- and allows entry and exit.

Burnout ends work.
Attrition sustains it.

---

## Resisting the Event Mentality

There will be no single reveal, moment, or reckoning.

- partial disclosures,
- incremental consequences,
- and uneven outcomes.

Progress measured in governance change outlasts spectacle.

---

## Learning Over Time

Slowness allows:
- correction,
- refinement,
- and adaptation.

Mistakes acknowledged early prevent systemic failure later.
