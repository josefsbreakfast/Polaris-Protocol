## Why Heat Is Inevitable

Accountability work attracts heat because it:
- threatens insulation,
- challenges narrative control,
- and imposes cost on avoidance.

Heat may arise from:
- media attention,
- institutional defensiveness,
- online hostility,
- internal disagreement,
- or sustained exposure to harm narratives.

Heat is a condition, not a failure.

---

## Heat Versus Momentum

Heat feels like momentum.
It is not.

Heat accelerates action.
Momentum sustains action.

Confusing the two leads to:
- rushed decisions,
- boundary collapse,
- and legitimacy loss.

This project prioritises momentum over intensity.

---

## Common Heat Signals

Warning signs include:
- pressure to act immediately,
- framing delay as cowardice,
- moral grandstanding,
- personalisation of critique,
- fixation on personalities,
- or disregard for agreed scope.

These signals indicate the need to slow down.

---

## Structural Cooling Measures

Cooling is structural, not emotional.

Effective measures include:
- returning to written principles,
- rechecking scope,
- pausing publication,
- deferring decisions,
- and rotating responsibility.

Cooling protects the work, not egos.

---

## Emotional Containment

Participants may experience:
- anger,
- grief,
- disgust,
- or helplessness.

These responses are understandable.

They are not decision tools.

When emotion spikes:
- step back,
- avoid public response,
- and resume after regulation.

Self-regulation is a collective responsibility.

---

## Provocation and Bait

Heat is often increased intentionally through:
- insults,
- misrepresentation,
- false urgency,
- or manufactured controversy.

Responding immediately rewards the tactic.

Non-response is not weakness.
It is refusal.

---

## Internal Disagreement

Disagreement is expected.

It becomes heat when:
- framed as moral failure,
- rushed to resolution,
- or resolved publicly.

Internal disagreement should be:
- documented,
- discussed slowly,
- and resolved procedurally.

Silencing disagreement creates later fracture.

---

## Public Attention Cycles

Public interest fluctuates.

Pressure to â€œcapitaliseâ€ on attention often leads to:
- overstatement,
- premature release,
- or scope creep.

This work is not time-sensitive theatre.
It is cumulative accountability.

Let attention rise and fall without chasing it.

---

## Protection of Survivors

Heat increases risk to survivors.

Escalation can:
- retraumatise,
- provoke harassment,
- or force unwanted visibility.

When heat rises, survivor protection tightens.

Survivor safety overrides urgency.

---

## Exit and Pause Permission

Participants may pause or exit at any time.

No explanation is required.
No loyalty is demanded.
No absence is moralised.

Sustainable work requires porous boundaries.

---

## Closing

Heat is not evidence of effectiveness.
It is evidence of pressure.

Managing heat preserves:
- judgement,
- legitimacy,
- and endurance.

Cooling is not retreat.
It is strategy.
