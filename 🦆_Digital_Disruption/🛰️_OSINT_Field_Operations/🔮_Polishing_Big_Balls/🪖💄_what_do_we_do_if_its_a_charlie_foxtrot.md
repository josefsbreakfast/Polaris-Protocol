## First: a reality check (important)

Before doing anything else, ask:

- Is anyone in immediate danger?  
- Is there evidence of active harm, not just poor process?  

In most cases, the answer is **no**.

A Charlie Foxtrot here usually means:
- chronic governance failure  
- misleading assurances  
- missing or retrofitted safeguards  
- hostile or inappropriate handling of lawful requests

## Option 1: Clarify and document (often enough)

Sometimes the most effective step is simply:
- restating the issue clearly  
- asking for confirmation in writing  
- requesting correction of the record  

This:
- creates an internal paper trail  
- gives institutions a chance to fix things quietly  
- often resolves more than expected  

If this works, you can stop here.

---

## Option 2: Use internal accountability routes

Public bodies usually have:
- a Data Protection Officer  
- an information governance lead  
- a complaints or assurance function  

A short, factual summary pointing to:
- missing documents  
- contradictory statements  
- governance gaps  

can be enough to trigger internal review.

You are not obliged to argue the case.  
Pointing to the paperwork is sufficient.

---

## Option 3: External regulators (still boring)

- you tried proportionate steps first  
- the issue is systemic  
- the concern is about governance, not grievance
