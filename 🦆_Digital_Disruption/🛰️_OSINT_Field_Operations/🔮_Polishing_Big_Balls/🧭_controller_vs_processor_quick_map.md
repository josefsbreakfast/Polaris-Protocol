When public bodies use external data services, two roles appear again and again: **data controller** and **data processor**.  
They sound technical. They are mostly practical.

Understanding the difference helps you:
- ask the right organisation the right question  
- avoid being bounced between entities  
- recognise when responsibility is being blurred  

This map keeps it simple.

---

## The controller: who decides

The **data controller** is the organisation that decides:
- *why* personal data is processed  
- *how* it is used in practice  
- *what decisions or actions* flow from it  

In public-sector contexts, this is usually:
- a government department  
- a local authority  
- an NHS body  
- a statutory agency  

The controller:
- defines the purpose  
- owns the risk  
- is responsible for legality  
- must ensure safeguards exist  

If something goes wrong, responsibility does not disappear just because a contractor was involved.

---

## The processor: who does the work

The **data processor** processes data *on behalf of* the controller.

This is often:
- a private company  
- a charity  
- a consortium member  
- a specialist analytics or IT supplier  

Processors:
- follow instructions  
- provide tools or services  
- may handle large volumes of data  
- may operate across many clients  

They do **not** decide the purpose on their own.  
They are meant to act within the limits set by the controller.
