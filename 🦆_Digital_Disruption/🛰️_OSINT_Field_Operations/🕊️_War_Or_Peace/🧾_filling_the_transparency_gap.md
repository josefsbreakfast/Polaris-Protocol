## üéì Context  

The transparency work documented here builds on **years of student and staff requests** for clarity about UK universities‚Äô financial and research relationships with arms manufacturers and, more specifically, with entities linked to the **Israeli occupation of Palestinian territory**.  
These demands did not begin with the Gaza escalation of October 2023; they form part of a longer campus ethics movement that asks universities to apply the same human-rights standards they promote in their marketing and teaching.  

At the University of Nottingham and elsewhere, students have repeatedly asked for:  
- publication of contracts and research partnerships with defence or surveillance companies;  
- disclosure of any commercial or academic arrangements with institutions operating in the Palestinian lands which remain under an illegal military occupation;  
- explanation of how such relationships align with universities‚Äô public commitments to human rights, environmental responsibility, and ethical investment.  

The **core grievance** is not merely political alignment, but **inconsistency of practice**: institutions present themselves as centres of humanitarian and environmental values while maintaining opaque financial or research ties that may contradict those values.  
After the **International Court of Justice‚Äôs provisional measures** on the Gaza situation (January 2024), students argued that transparency was no longer optional‚Äîit was a **legal and moral requirement** under the Genocide Convention‚Äôs duty to prevent harm.  

This node therefore situates the current OSINT work inside that longer accountability tradition: a civic request for evidence, not a campaign of hostility.  

It is also noted throughout this section of Polaris, that universities do not exist as institutions without multiple stakeholders and influences; the proposed OSINT tasks therefore look at a broad range of factors which may contribute to a given university's threat or risk assessment, when confronted with demands for transparency, and what may motivate the current positioning of many UK universities in the current time.

## üß© 1. Contractual & Financial Transparency Layer  

| Missing or Obscured Item | What It Would Show | How It Normally Appears |
|---------------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| **Defence & security contract lists** | Named partners (e.g., BAE, Rolls-Royce, Elbit), project titles, contract values, durations | Annual procurement reports, FOI disclosures, Companies House filings |
| **Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs)** | Formal R&D or data-sharing agreements with defence or Israeli institutions | Partnership announcements, Senate or Council minutes |
| **Tender / bid documentation** | Which labs bid for UKRI, Innovate UK, or Home Office security grants | Contracts Finder, ResearchFish, Gateway to Research |
| **Commercialisation agreements (NTV / incubator)** | Spin-outs licensing or selling IP relevant to surveillance or defence | Nottingham Technology Ventures annual reports |
| **Charitable donations & endowments** | Donor origins and purpose of funds | University financial statements and gift registers |

**Gap meaning:** absence of these records prevents tracing the money and decision-making that link humanitarian branding with military funding streams.

## üîç 6. Methods to Begin Filling the Gap (Open & Ethical)  

1. **FOI / EIR** ‚Äî Request aggregated defence-funding data, partnership lists, DPIAs, and redacted committee minutes.  
2. **UKRI Gateway to Research mining** ‚Äî Filter by security, dual-use, counter-terrorism, or Israel tags; export results to CSV.  
3. **Companies House & incubator filings** ‚Äî Identify spin-outs, shareholders, and PSC overlaps.  
4. **Export-control database search** ‚Äî Cross-check university entities against ECJU public licence lists.  
5. **Minutes & board papers via FOI** ‚Äî Test whether ethics boards discussed security collaborations.  
6. **Community-impact mapping** ‚Äî Match contract or grant dates with protest crackdowns and media coverage.  

Each task can be performed entirely within open-source and legal bounds.

### üß≠ Ethical Framing for Contributors  

This OSINT stream focuses on **policing, security, and disciplinary transparency**.  
All work must remain within open, public sources: published records, archived web pages, procurement databases, and lawful FOI or EIR requests.  
**Do not** attempt intrusion or unauthorised access.  
The purpose is civic verification ‚Äî to illuminate governance and data-flow structures so that universities, governments, and students can share a common factual ground.  

By widening the field of investigators beyond those directly affected, outside contributors also **reduce the personal risk borne by students** and help distribute the evidential burden across the wider community.
