## ğŸ•¯ï¸ 1.  Kavod ha-Met â€” Dignity of the Dead  

> â€œTo bury the dead and to speak truth of them are one commandment.â€ â€” Rabbinic principle

In Jewish practice, *kavod ha-met* demands presence, honesty, and care: the body is not property but a trust.  
Governments and institutions inherit that same duty when death occurs in their service or under their watch.

**Core obligations**
1. Treat remains and memory as inviolable.  
2. Notify families swiftly and truthfully.  
3. Refrain from political manipulation of casualty data.  
4. Support witnesses and investigators seeking clarity.

Deaths of UN or allied peacekeepers â€” e.g., **Irish UNIFIL troops in Lebanon (2022)** â€” illustrate how ambiguity breeds suspicion.  
Families and colleagues repeatedly report delays, partial disclosures, or contradictory accounts.

**Minimum standard:**  
- Immediate notification with preliminary facts;  
- Public confirmation once next of kin informed;  
- Independent inquiry within six months;  
- Publication of findings except where protection of witnesses demands redaction.

Transparency honours service; secrecy erodes consent.
