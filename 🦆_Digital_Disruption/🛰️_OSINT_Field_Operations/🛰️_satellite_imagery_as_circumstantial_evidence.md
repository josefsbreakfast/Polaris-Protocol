## ğŸ›°ï¸ Orientation

This node defines the **proper evidentiary role of satellite imagery** in human-rights, conflict, and governance investigations.

Satellite imagery is not used to prove individual acts of violence in isolation. Its value lies in **corroboration, anomaly detection, and burden-shifting** when physical access, records, or testimony are restricted.

In closed or rights-void systems, imagery often becomes one of the few remaining independent observation channels.

## ğŸ§­ Evidentiary Status: What Satellite Imagery Is (and Is Not)

### What It Is

* Circumstantial evidence
* Corroborative signal
* Temporal change detector
* Access-denial validator

### What It Is Not

* Direct proof of specific crimes
* Standalone forensic confirmation
* Substitute for witness testimony

Misuse occurs when imagery is treated as dispositive rather than contextual.

## ğŸ§© How Imagery Enters Serious Investigations

In academic, legal, and OSINT practice, satellite imagery is typically used to:

1. **Establish Baselines**
   Document normal conditions prior to a policy shift, transfer, or operational change.

2. **Detect Temporal Anomalies**
   Identify changes in terrain, structures, surface patterns, or activity over time.

3. **Corroborate Testimony**
   Support or challenge witness accounts without replacing them.

4. **Flag Sites for Inspection**
   Justify access demands or moratoria when anomalies coincide with restricted access.

5. **Shift the Burden of Explanation**
   Require custodial authorities to explain changes when independent verification is blocked.

## ğŸ›°ï¸ Resolution and Modality Constraints

Publicly accessible imagery (e.g. Google Earth, Google Maps) has significant limits:

* low spatial resolution
* irregular update intervals
* limited spectral bands

Higher-grade analysis typically relies on:

* commercial providers (e.g. Maxar, Planet)
* multi-temporal sequences
* multispectral or thermal bands

Failure to acknowledge resolution limits leads to overclaiming.
