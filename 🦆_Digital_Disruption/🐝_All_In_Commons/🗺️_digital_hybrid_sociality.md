## ğŸ¾ Lineage: Scavenger Hunts, Correspondence, and Artifact-Led Sociality

Across the early internet and into the present, a recurring structure appears:

- scavenger hunts and discovery systems  
- artifact-based correspondence  
- object- or place-centred tracking  
- asynchronous participation  
- optional documentation rather than compulsory broadcasting  

Examples include geocaching, postcard exchanges, and book-passing systems.

These systems succeed when they:
- track **things, places, or traces**, not people  
- tolerate uneven or episodic participation  
- avoid real-time disclosure  
- allow private meaning  
- scale *down* as well as up  

They fracture when identity performance, optimisation, or extraction becomes central.

## ğŸ§­ Territory, Not Property

Humans are territorial animals â€” not in the sense of ownership, but in the sense of **embodied orientation**.

- where movement is familiar  
- where patterns repeat  
- where memory accumulates  
- where the body can down-regulate  

As private living space compresses, **public and shared spaces must compensate**.

- extending the felt boundaries of home

This reframes the importance of:

- walkable neighbourhoods

This is not lifestyle politics.
