# ⚖️ Legal Flagging Incident Report  
**First created:** 2025-11-02 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-20  
*Minimal reporting template for escalating linguistic-incitement or hate-speech events to legal or advocacy partners.*

---

## 🧭 Orientation  

This template standardises how potential legal incidents are logged across the Polaris ecosystem.  
Use it when a phrase, post, or coordinated campaign meets thresholds for **incitement**, **harassment**, or **hate speech** under applicable law.  
The goal: reproducible evidence + clear chain of custody.

---

## 🧾 Required Metadata  

| Field | Description |
|-------|--------------|
| **Incident ID** | Unique tag: `LIR-YYYY-MM-DD-###` |
| **Date / Time (UTC)** | When event captured |
| **Platform / Channel** | e.g. Twitter, Telegram, Broadcast |
| **Actor(s)** | Known usernames, handles, or organisations |
| **Phrase / Term Flagged** | Exact wording (verbatim) |
| **Linked Watchlist ID** | e.g. `df-0001` |
| **Nature of Violation** | incitement / harassment / hate speech / threat |
| **Harm Context** | Targeted group / geographic scope |
| **Evidence Bundle** | Path to capture set (screenshots + hashes) |
| **Cross-Mirrors** | Mirror-A / Mirror-B |
| **Initial Reviewer** | Name / role |
| **Escalation Path** | NGO / Legal counsel / Regulator |
| **Outcome** | Pending / Referred / Actioned |

---

---

## ⚙️ Submission Protocol  

1. Verify the phrase exists in the Watchlist (`df-####`).  
2. Confirm ≥3 corroborating examples within 48 h.  
3. Archive and hash all materials (use Archive Capture Template).  
4. Fill report form; submit to legal partner via encrypted channel.  
5. Log submission in `legal/escalation_register.csv`.  
6. Retain confirmation receipt.

## 🧱 Legal References (UK context, adaptable internationally)  

- **Public Order Act 1986**, Part III — incitement to racial hatred.  
- **Communications Act 2003**, s.127 — grossly offensive or menacing messages.  
- **Online Safety Act (2023)** — platform obligations for removal and cooperation.  
- **Human Rights Act 1998**, Article 10 — freedom of expression balance test.
