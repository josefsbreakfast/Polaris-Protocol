- This folder replaces the earlier вҖңlensesвҖқ folder.

- The purpose is to show how to review the `рҹҢҲ_Gold_Pot_Chasers` problem from different analytical points of view.

- The key claim:
  - complex systems need more than one analytical viewpoint;
  - no single lens should be treated as the whole object;
  - reviewing point of view is part of anti-overclaiming discipline.
- This folder should help the reader ask:
  - what does this look like as containment?
  - what does this look like as authority and legitimacy?
  - what does this look like as reflexive risk cartography?

## рҹ’« Why вҖңReviewing POVвҖқ

- вҖңLensesвҖқ was structurally accurate but slightly too static.
- `Reviewing POV` makes the method more active.
- A lens can sound like a fixed interpretive frame.
- A reviewing point of view is something the reader can move into, test, and move out of.
- The point is not:
  - pick one favourite theory;
  - force every example through it;
  - make the system fit the lens.
- The point is:
  - review from one angle;
  - notice what becomes visible;
  - notice what remains hidden;
  - review again from another angle;
  - keep the object larger than the method.

## рҹ§Ҡ Containment Studies POV

- The containment studies point of view asks:
  - what is being contained?
  - who is being contained?
  - what story is being contained?
  - what evidence is being contained?
  - what risk is being displaced?
  - what pressure is being absorbed by the wrong person or group?
- Useful for reviewing:
  - public-order systems;
  - safeguarding failures;
  - reputational management;
  - platform suppression;
  - institutional defensiveness;
  - misclassification;
  - вҖңrisk objectвҖқ formation.
- This POV should help identify when a system is not solving the fault but containing the person who makes the fault visible.
- Key warning:
  - containment can look like safety while protecting the system from accountability.

## рҹ§„ Exousiological Dynamics POV

- The exousiological dynamics point of view asks how authority works.
- It reviews:
  - legitimacy;
  - power;
  - permission;
  - delegated authority;
  - institutional confidence;
  - jurisdiction;
  - who gets believed;
  - who gets treated as dangerous;
  - who can define the problem.
- Useful for reviewing:
  - government authority;
  - procurement authority;
  - vendor authority;
  - policing authority;
  - safeguarding authority;
  - intelligence-adjacent authority;
  - platform authority;
  - diaspora and community legitimacy.
- This POV should help identify when a systemвҖҷs formal authority is not matched by practical legitimacy.
- Key warning:
  - authority-coded does not mean technically current;
  - lawful power does not automatically mean well-calibrated judgement.

## рҹҰ‘ CRAKEN Cartography POV

- The CRAKEN cartography point of view maps reflexive risk.
- It asks:
  - what risks are being created by the attempt to manage risk?
  - what signals are fed back into the system?
  - who becomes more visible because they tried to document harm?
  - what new pressure appears because a person appealed, published, corrected, evidenced, or resisted?
  - what happens when the system reads defence as escalation?
- Useful for reviewing:
  - automated risk systems;
  - platform feedback loops;
  - evidence-generation risk;
  - publication as signal;
  - disability-access dependence;
  - public-order misclassification;
  - actor pathways;
  - recursive institutional fear.
- This POV should help identify when a systemвҖҷs own protective logic produces new exposure.
- Key warning:
  - a system can create the risk it claims to measure.

## рҹ§ӯ How To Apply Reviewing POV Without Overclaiming

- This folder needs a method node for using these perspectives safely.
- The rule:
  - a point of view is a review position, not a verdict.
- A reviewer should be able to say:
  - from a containment studies POV, this looks like containment;
  - from an exousiological dynamics POV, this looks like authority-legitimacy mismatch;
  - from a CRAKEN cartography POV, this looks like recursive risk production;
  - but the record still has to support the claim.
- Reviewing POV should never replace evidence.
- It should help organise questions.
- It should make audit sharper, not looser.
- The point is not to win the interpretation.
- The point is to make better distinctions.

## рҹ§Ә Shared Method Across POVs

- Each POV should ask:
  - what becomes visible from this angle?
  - what becomes less visible?
  - what evidence supports this reading?
  - what evidence would weaken this reading?
  - what would a hostile or careless reader overclaim here?
  - what audit question follows?
  - what repair route follows?
- Each POV should separate:
  - observation;
  - interpretation;
  - hypothesis;
  - audit question;
  - claim.
- This folder should reinforce the clusterвҖҷs wider discipline:
  - map the vulnerability;
  - identify who could use it;
  - identify what they would gain;
  - identify who carries the risk;
  - identify what safeguards would reduce exposure;
  - do not claim more than the evidence supports.

## рҹ§ұ Relationship To The Shared Exploit Surface

- The shared exploit surface is the object being reviewed.
- Reviewing POVs help inspect that object from different angles.
- Containment studies may reveal:
  - who or what is being contained instead of repaired.
- Exousiological dynamics may reveal:
  - how authority, legitimacy, and permission shape the system.
- CRAKEN cartography may reveal:
  - how risk-management systems produce new risks.
- None of these POVs is the whole object.
- Together, they help prevent the analysis from becoming too flat.

## рҹ•іпёҸ Relationship To Public Legibility

- Reviewing POV helps with public legibility.
- A public reader may recognise a scandal but not the system.
- A reviewer using these POVs can show:
  - where containment is happening;
  - where authority is miscalibrated;
  - where the system produces recursive risk;
  - where the public lacks a category for the overlap.
- This makes the structure easier to explain without turning the explanation into accusation soup.
- The aim is not to make the reader believe everything at once.
- The aim is to give the reader better ways to look.

## вҡ–пёҸ Boundary Discipline

- This folder should not become theory theatre.
- It should not use specialised terms to make weak claims sound stronger.
- It should not treat every system as containment.
- It should not treat every authority problem as illegitimate power.
- It should not treat every feedback loop as malicious targeting.
- It should say:
  - this is what the point of view helps us notice;
  - this is what the evidence supports;
  - this is what remains uncertain;
  - this is the audit question that follows.
- The POV is a tool.
- The record is still the anchor.
