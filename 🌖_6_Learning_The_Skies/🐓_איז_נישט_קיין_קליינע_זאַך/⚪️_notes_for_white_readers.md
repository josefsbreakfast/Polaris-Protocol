## 🛰️ Orientation  

As a Jew who is also white, the author is not saying there is nothing to say to Jewish men.

There is plenty to say.

There is also plenty to say to Jewish women, Israeli institutions, diaspora institutions, defence-tech actors, lawyers, investors, activists, public officials, community leaders, and anyone else sitting inside these systems.

But white Western publics are also part of the problem.

White British, white American, white German, French, European, settler-colonial, imperial, NATO-adjacent, finance-adjacent, university-adjacent, media-adjacent, and defence-adjacent publics need to stop treating Jewish visibility as a convenient substitute for examining their own states, economies, militaries, banks, universities, media systems, defence industries, and colonial inheritances.

The immediate problem is not being sustained by the State of Israel alone.  

> Guys: we are not secretly ruling the entire Western financial and deep state systems. Examine your own ingrained core beliefs, please.   

It is supported, financed, armed, justified, normalised, abstracted, delayed, and administratively managed through many other countries and institutions.

That means Jewish men are not the only people who need scrutiny.

They are not the only people with power.

They are not the only people with proximity.

They are not the only people whose decisions matter.

And yet, in this particular bridge sequence, some Jewish men and Jewish women may need to be spoken to through Jewish relational, ethical, scholarly, and communal routes.

That requires space.

## 🕯️ The Boundary  

If you are reading this as a white person, and you are already inside this folder, please understand that this space was not created for you to centre yourself.

You may be here because you are trying to centre Palestinians.

You may be here because you are trying to centre the people who are more racialised, colonised, occupied, or structurally harmed by coercive systems, than Jewish people.

You may be doing that sincerely.

This note is not saying otherwise.

It is saying that, in this specific set of notes, there needs to be room for a Jewish intra-community conversation to happen.

Without that room, the author cannot see a viable path for Jewish communities, Jewish institutions, Jewish professionals, or Jewish people under pressure to move toward the kind of engagement many white witnesses say they want: protection of Palestinian life, serious attention to international humanitarian law, resistance to dehumanisation, and a better relationship with the rights of racialised and colonised people more generally.

This is not a request for permanent silence.

It is not immunity.

It is not the suspension of critique everywhere.

It is a request for space inside this specific bridge.

The author is acknowledging that there is a great deal behind the position Jewish people currently occupy as both oppressed and, in some contexts, implicated in oppression.

That complexity does not disappear.

It is being held here deliberately.

But for this moment, the request is:

> Please hold it for long enough that we can talk to them.

This does not mean you cannot criticise the author elsewhere.

It does not mean you cannot say, in another context, that this work is insufficient, incomplete, mistaken, too cautious, too sharp, too Jewish, not Jewish enough, not Palestinian-centred enough, or not politically aligned with you.

It means that this particular folder is trying to build an intra-community bridge under conditions where that bridge has already been strained by white institutions, white publics, white witness-positioning, and white allyship-performance.  

Some of that pressure has come from people outside the Jewish community, whi believe they are protecting Jews.  

Some of that pressure has come from people who believe they are protecting Palestinians.  

Some of it has come from people who believe they are protecting other racialised or colonised groups.

Some of it has come from people who believe they are defending liberal institutions, public safety, democracy, anti-racism, or international law.

Intentions vary.

The effect still matters.

In the author’s experience, white institutional and white public interventions have often come between Jewish communities and our ability to speak honestly enough with one another, in order to change behaviour on these matters.

That is part of the problem this note is naming.

If you want better outcomes, please pause before inserting yourself into this intra-community conversation.

For this moment, the request is simpler:

> Let us build the bridge.

Because we have to be honest:  

> we centre ourselves all the time as white people, and it is not necessarilly clear why white supervision of Jewish discourse needs to happen. Jewish people do not need to be uniquely parented, we do not need to be uniquely cared for in a way that means there is no legal accountability, and I don't think Palestine liberation is hanging on you as a white person checking that the Jews aren't schemeing.
> 
> Examine your own biases first, before interrupting our conversational space, about what we're going to do to improve our behaviour.
