Learning The Skies contains the teaching and translation layer of Polaris.

## 📚 Narrative Teaching & Fictionalisation  

Some modules within this cluster may be:
- fictional,
- allegorical,
- satirical,
- symbolic,
- or composite narratives.

These entries will always be clearly marked.

This follows longstanding human teaching traditions including:
- folklore,
- fairy tales,
- oral storytelling,
- political satire,
- trickster narratives,
- parables,
- and fables.

Narrative compression can help humans:
- recognise patterns,
- metabolise complexity,
- discuss difficult systems safely,
- and retain operational lessons more effectively.

These stories are teaching instruments.  
Not deceptive reconstructions.

## 🧩 Core Teaching Principles  

This cluster operates on several recurring principles:
- humans learn through stories,
- humour lowers defensive resistance,
- operational reality matters,
- embodied expertise matters,
- and systems fail when translation layers collapse.

Particular attention is given to:
- tacit knowledge,
- frontline experience,
- disability knowledge,
- care labour,
- healthcare culture,
- and “unprestigious” operational expertise.
