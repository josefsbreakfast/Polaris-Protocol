# 🍷 Always Spiritually In Jerusalem  
**First created:** 2026-05-25 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-25  
*A symbolic and diasporic subcluster exploring Jerusalem, exile, longing, orientation, and continuity outside compulsory geopolitical reduction.*  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

**🍷 Always Spiritually In Jerusalem** is a subcluster within **☁️ Kabbalist Futurism** dedicated to the symbolic, mystical, emotional, ritual, and diasporic meanings attached to:  
- Jerusalem,  
- exile,  
- return,  
- longing,  
- orientation,  
- homeland,  
- covenant,  
- memory,  
- and Jewish continuity across time and geography.  

This cluster acknowledges openly that these subjects exist in unavoidable proximity to modern political conflict.  
Jewish history, ritual, liturgy, symbolism, and collective memory cannot be cleanly separated from conversations surrounding Zion, exile, nationhood, diaspora, or the modern State of Israel.  

However:  

This subcluster is **not** intended to function primarily as a geopolitical debate space.  

The Polaris Protocol already contains extensive political writing elsewhere.  
Readers seeking the author's political analysis may explore other areas of the repository independently.  

This room exists for something else.  

It exists because many Jewish people are exhausted by the total saturation of symbolic, spiritual, historical, and emotional life by permanent catastrophe-processing and ideological sorting.  

There must remain places where people can:  
- study symbolism,  
- explore ritual meaning,  
- discuss exile and return,  
- compare diasporic experiences,  
- examine mystical traditions,  
- argue interpretively,  
- share stories,  
- make diagrams,  
- laugh,  
- mourn,  
- pray or not pray,  
- and remain recognisably human to one another  

without every conversation immediately collapsing into loyalty testing or geopolitical escalation.  

This cluster therefore deliberately prioritises:  
- emotional sustainability,  
- interpretive openness,  
- symbolic literacy,  
- layered historical consciousness,  
- and diasporic multiplicity.  

Readers within this space may hold radically different political positions.  
The existence of the space does not imply ideological agreement.  

Instead, the shared premise is simpler:  

Jewish symbolic and emotional life must remain larger than any single political frame.

---

## 🧿 Scope & Boundary  

This subcluster is intentionally bounded.  

Political realities are not denied here.  
Nor are grief, violence, displacement, or conflict treated as unreal or unimportant.  

However, the purpose of this space is not to force every symbolic or spiritual discussion into immediate contemporary political adjudication.  

The boundary exists because communities under chronic stress require spaces where:  
- imagination survives,  
- humour survives,  
- symbolic thinking survives,  
- and people can encounter one another without permanent nervous-system escalation.  

This is a continuity space, not a tribunal.
