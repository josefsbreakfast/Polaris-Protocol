---

## 🛰️ Orientation  
Containment and détente are not moral opposites but **temporal instruments**.  
They manage *speed* and *scope* — the tempo of change and the range of permitted relation.  
When viewed as temporal governance, containment slows, filters, and sequences; détente widens and synchronises.  

---

## ✨ Core Principles  

- **Rate Control:** regulating how quickly novelty can enter a system.  
- **Scope Control:** determining how far interactions can extend without collapse.  
- **Temporal Asymmetry:** power lies in controlling others’ clocks — their reaction time, refresh cycles, and update velocity.  
- **Phase Windows:** moments of détente are scheduled relief intervals that maintain system survivability.

---

## 🕰️ Implication  
Temporal governance converts energy into order.  
When external dynamics exceed the system’s time constants, governance fails — not from lack of will, but from **temporal mismatch**.
