# 🧿 Temporal Metadata Ghosts  
**When Systems Refuse to Let the Past Become Past**  
**First created:** 2025-12-29 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-20  
*On time collapse, stale signal persistence, and structural re-harm via metadata*

---

## 🛰️ Orientation

This node defines a distinct failure mode within the Polaris ghost taxonomy:  
**Temporal Metadata Ghosts**.

These are not haunting returns of memory or identity, and not merely corrupted or duplicated records.  
They are **metadata artefacts produced when systems fail to respect temporal boundaries**, causing *past information to be treated as present signal*.

The harm arises not from remembering incorrectly, but from **refusing to let time do its job**.

---

## I. Definition

A **Temporal Metadata Ghost** is a residual operational signal that:

- originates in valid past data  
- remains structurally intact  
- survives its legitimate relevance window  
- and continues to influence present decisions  

The ghost exists because the system:
- lacks decay logic,
- collapses historical and current states,
- or federates signals without temporal context.

---

## II. How Temporal Ghosts Differ from Other Ghost Types

### 🪦 Haunting Ghosts
- Narrative return
- Old selves resurfacing
- Memory, meaning, identity
- “The past refuses to stay past”

### 👾 Metadata Ghosts
- Record corruption
- Duplicates and contradictions
- Administrative residue
- “The record will not die”

### 🧿 Temporal Metadata Ghosts (this node)
- Time misclassification
- Stale signals treated as live
- Resolved risk treated as active threat
- “The system refuses to move on”

This is **not memory**.  
It is **temporal negligence encoded as infrastructure**.

---

## III. Common Sources of Temporal Metadata Ghosts

Temporal ghosts arise when:

- safeguarding flags never decay  
- risk scores lack expiry  
- “historical” markers are ignored by analytics layers  
- resolved cases remain inputs to models  
- attention spikes revive old signals  
- federation collapses timestamps into correlation  

The data is not wrong.  
Its **time context is missing**.

---

## IV. Why Survivors Are Disproportionately Harmed

Survivors of abuse, violence, or coercion are more likely to have:

- long-lived records  
- safeguarding annotations  
- cross-institutional data trails  
- episodic reactivation of documentation  

When temporal ghosts persist:

- healed individuals are treated as ongoing risk  
- past trauma is operationalised as present instability  
- vulnerability is treated as permanent  

This produces **secondary harm by design**, not by intent.

---

## V. Federation as a Time-Collapse Engine

Federated systems are especially dangerous here.

One system records:
- “historical safeguarding involvement”

Another records:
- “behavioural anomaly”

A third records:
- “engagement spike”

The federated layer sees:
- **signal**

Time collapses.

At that point:
- past ≈ present  
- resolution ≈ suspicion  
- recovery ≈ anomaly  

This is how temporal ghosts scale from individual harm to systemic bias.

Temporal Metadata Ghosts engage multiple legal failures:

- **Equality Act (indirect discrimination)**

The system continues to act on information **after its lawful justification has expired**.

In plain terms:  
**people are punished for having a past**.

## VII. Why This Is Not a PR or Training Problem

No amount of:
- messaging  
- comms strategy  
- staff awareness  
can fix a temporal ghost.

This is a **systems design problem**.

Temporal ghosts persist until:
- expiry logic is enforced  
- decay is audited  
- federation is constrained  
- and historical data is contextually firewalled
