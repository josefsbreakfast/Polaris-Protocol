This node defines **waiting-as-containment** as a governance condition in which a subject is held in procedural suspension without charge, disclosure, or horizon.

Waiting here is not patience, pause, or delay.  
It is the **active mechanism** by which control is maintained without overt action.

Silence functions as process.  

Endurance is substituted for resolution.  

> "He was living in a free country, after all, everywhere was at peace, all laws were decent and were upheld, who was it who dared accost him in his own home?"

Containment is often imagined as force, exclusion, or punishment.  
This node documents a quieter form: **indefinite procedural waiting**.

In this mode:
- no accusation is issued  
- no rule is formally broken  
- no appeal is made available  
- no endpoint is declared

They are **held**.

Responsibility shifts inward:

## ğŸ¥€ Core Theory: Waiting-as-Containment

Waiting-as-containment has five defining properties:

1. **No Stated Trigger**  
   The subject is never told what initiated the process.

2. **No Stated Endpoint**  
   Time passes without criteria for completion.

3. **No Legible Authority**  
   Decisions appear to occur elsewhere, without named agents.

4. **Silence as Procedure**  
   Non-response is not absence of action; it *is* the action.

5. **Endurance Recast as Responsibility**  
   Stability, restraint, and patience are demanded of the subject, while the system remains opaque.

Waiting is not a gap between events.  
**Waiting is the event.**  

> "If you take our advice, you won't tire yourself out thinking about things to no purpose, you need to pull yourself together as there's a lot that's going to required of you.
> You've not behaved towards us the way we deserve after being so good to you, you forget that we, whatever we are, we're still free men and you're not, and that's quite an advantage."
