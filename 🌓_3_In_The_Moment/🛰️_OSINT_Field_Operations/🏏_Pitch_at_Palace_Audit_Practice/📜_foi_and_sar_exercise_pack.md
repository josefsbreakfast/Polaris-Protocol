# 📜 FOI + SAR Exercise Pack — Pitch @ Palace Audit Practice  
**First created:** 2025-11-02 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-21  
*Practical transparency exercises for examining prestige-linked public interfaces through lawful information requests.*  

---

## 🧭 Orientation  

This node translates the Pitch @ Palace case into a **transparency exercise pack**.

It demonstrates how to request information **lawfully, politely, and persistently** using:
- the Freedom of Information Act 2000 (FOIA), and  
- Subject Access Requests (SARs) under UK GDPR.

It does **not** assume wrongdoing.  
It assumes curiosity — and that democratic citizens are entitled to understand how prestige networks interact with public institutions.

---

## 🧩 Why This Matters  

Open-source investigation works best when it is lawful and boring.

FOIs and SARs are not weapons.  
They are instruments of **civic calibration**.

Every exemption, delay, or “information not held” response generates metadata.  
That metadata reveals where **institutional resistance**, sensitivity, or opacity tends to cluster.

## 🗂️ Part I — FOI Templates  

Each template can be adapted by date range or department.

Guidelines:
- keep tone neutral  
- cite FOIA 2000 explicitly  
- log every reference number  
- expect exemptions as data, not failure  

---

### 🏛️ 1. Department for Business & Trade (DBT / ex-DIT / UKTI)

```
Subject: FOI Request – Pitch @ Palace Engagements 2016–2019

Please provide copies of:

• Briefing notes, memoranda of understanding, or correspondence between
DBT and Pitch @ Palace Ltd or Pitch @ Palace CIC.

• Any internal guidance concerning royal involvement in trade-promotion
or entrepreneurial support activities.

• Any risk assessments or due-diligence checks relating to event
sponsorship or partner organisations.
```

---

### 🌍 2. Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO)

```
Please provide copies of internal briefings, cables, or correspondence
referencing overseas Pitch @ Palace events (including Asia, Gulf states,
Australia, or Latin America) between 2014 and 2019.
```

---

### 💼 3. HM Treasury / OFSI

```
Please provide records of any sanctions screening, AML reviews, or
related assessments concerning payments to or from Pitch @ Palace Ltd
or Pitch @ Palace CIC between 2014 and 2020.
```

---

### 🏦 4. HMRC (AML Supervision)

```
Please confirm whether any Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) were filed
that reference Pitch @ Palace Ltd or Pitch @ Palace CIC.
```

---

### 🧮 5. Cabinet Office

```
Please provide any policy, guidance, or advisory documents concerning
the permitted use of royal branding or association in quasi-commercial
or entrepreneurial programmes.
```

---

### 🧠 6. Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO)

```
Please provide details of any complaints, enquiries, or investigations
involving Pitch @ Palace-related entities or events between 2014 and 2020.
```

Each response — material, partial, or refused — is analytically useful.

## 📬 Part II — Subject Access Request (SAR) Templates  

SARs target **data controllers** who may hold your personal data, including:
- registration details  
- photographs or video  
- correspondence  

Cite **Article 15 UK GDPR** and provide sufficient identifying information.

---

### 🧾 Template A — Pitch @ Palace Ltd / CIC (or liquidator)

```
Under Article 15 UK GDPR and section 45 Data Protection Act 2018,
I request copies of all personal data relating to me collected during
Pitch @ Palace events at [venue/date].

This includes registration records, imagery, correspondence, and
internal notes.

Please state the lawful basis for processing and current data-retention status.
```

---

### 🏫 Template B — Host Universities

```
Please provide all personal data you hold relating to my attendance at
the Pitch @ Palace event hosted at [institution/date], including
registration forms, consent records, and imagery.

Please identify any third-party recipients of this data.
```

---

### 💼 Template C — Corporate Sponsors

```
Please confirm whether Pitch @ Palace shared any attendee data with you.

If so, please provide:
• categories of personal data received
• purpose of processing
• retention period
```

---

### 📸 Template D — Media or Press Agencies

```
Please provide copies of any images or footage identifying me captured
during Pitch @ Palace events, together with details of licensing or
distribution arrangements.
```

## 🧭 Recording and Mapping Responses  

Maintain a simple tracking spreadsheet:

| Request ID | Date Sent | Target | Type | Status | Exemptions | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 001 | 2025-11-03 | DBT | FOI | Pending | — | Reference logged |

Clusters of exemptions — for example:
- s.23 (National Security)  
- s.37 (Royal Communications)  
- s.40 (Personal Data)  

can be visualised to map **where resistance concentrates**.

and more about observing **how power behaves when asked politely**.
