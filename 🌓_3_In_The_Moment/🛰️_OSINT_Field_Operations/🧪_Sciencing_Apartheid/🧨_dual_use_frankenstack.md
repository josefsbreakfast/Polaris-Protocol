## 💸 Economic Inversion — When Surveillance Masquerades as Efficiency  

Top-down “innovation” projects in health and care often look efficient on paper but waste resources in practice. Most funds flow into monitoring infrastructure, vendor licences, and data-extraction layers, while the therapeutic interface — human time, trust, and continuity — is under-funded.  
Community- or patient-led initiatives reverse that ratio: they spend on what works because they know what hurts.  

| Design locus | Spend profile | Typical outcome | Efficiency reality |
|:--------------|:--------------|:----------------|:------------------|
| **Vendor-driven / top-down** | 70–80 % on tech stack, consultants, dashboards | High data throughput, low patient satisfaction | *Looks* efficient via KPIs, but produces dependency and waste |
| **Community- or patient-led** | 70–80 % on local capacity, peer staff, repair loops | High trust, lower attrition, better health metrics | Fewer sunk costs, self-sustaining over time |

> **True fiscal efficiency** = funding designs that people will actually use and trust.  
> When care becomes a data-harvesting exercise, the public pays twice — once for the tech, and again for the mistrust it breeds.
