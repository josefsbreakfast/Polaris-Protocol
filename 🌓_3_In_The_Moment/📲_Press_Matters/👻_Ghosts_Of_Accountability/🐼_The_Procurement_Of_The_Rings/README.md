## 🛰️ Orientation

This cluster tracks the public-interest implications of the dispute between Palantir, the Metropolitan Police, and the Mayor’s Office for Policing and Crime.

This is not a pro-Palantir argument.

It is not a simple anti-Palantir argument either.

The stranger possibility is that a controversial private contractor, acting from commercial self-interest, may force disclosure around public-sector governance failures that ordinary citizens, wrongly arrested people, harmed complainants, women failed by stalking systems, and bereaved families often cannot afford to expose.

The tulpa moves for tulpa reasons.

The public may still get light through the crack.
