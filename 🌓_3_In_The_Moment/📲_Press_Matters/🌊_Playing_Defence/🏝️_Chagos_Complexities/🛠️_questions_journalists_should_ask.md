🛠️# 🛠️ Questions Journalists Should Ask  
First created: 2026-06-02 | Last updated: 2026-06-02  
Press-facing checklist for reporting Chagos without collapsing sovereignty, base access, contractors, India, AI/space infrastructure, and media pressure into one thin frame.

---

## 🧭 Orientation

Chagos coverage needs better questions.

Not because journalists are failing as individuals.

Because the story sits inside a fused pressure field:

- colonial history,
- Chagossian displacement,
- Mauritian sovereignty,
- UK–US military continuity,
- India’s Indian Ocean strategy,
- China anxiety,
- AUKUS and Indo-Pacific security architecture,
- defence contractors,
- AI, space, telecoms, and data infrastructure,
- media pressure,
- and public accountability.

A rushed article can easily become someone else’s frame.

This checklist is for slowing the frame down before publication.

---

## 🏝️ Naming And Frame Questions

1. Are we saying Chagos, Diego Garcia, or both?

2. Have we explained that Chagos is the wider archipelago, while Diego Garcia is the largest island and the site of the UK–US base?

3. Does the headline over-centre the military base and erase the archipelago?

4. Does the headline over-centre sovereignty and fail to explain the base?

5. Does the article make clear whether it is discussing:
   - the archipelago,
   - the base,
   - the treaty,
   - Chagossian rights,
   - Mauritius sovereignty,
   - UK–US military access,
   - or contractor interests?

6. Which actor benefits from the wording we have chosen?

7. What disappears if the story is framed only as “Diego Garcia”?

8. What disappears if the story is framed only as “Chagos”?

---

## 👣 Chagossian Rights Questions

1. Are Chagossian voices included directly?

2. Are Chagossian interests being treated separately from Mauritian state interests?

3. Does the piece explain displacement, exclusion, return, remedy, memory, and dignity?

4. Does the article ask whether Chagossians have meaningful participation in future governance?

5. Does it explain whether any right of return exists, where, and under what limits?

6. Does it explain whether Diego Garcia remains excluded from return or civilian settlement?

7. Does it avoid treating Chagossians as symbolic background to a state-to-state settlement?

8. Does the piece risk repeating the old logic: people becoming inconvenient around strategic infrastructure?

---

## 🏛️ Mauritius Questions

1. What exactly does Mauritius gain under the treaty or proposed framework?

2. What sovereignty rights does Mauritius exercise?

3. What rights are carved out for UK use of Diego Garcia?

4. What oversight, consultation, or assurance mechanisms does Mauritius receive?

5. How does the settlement affect environmental governance, marine protection, policing, civil jurisdiction, and future renegotiation?

6. Is Mauritius being treated as a sovereign actor or as a complication around a base?

7. Are we distinguishing Mauritius’s interests from India’s, the UK’s, the US’s, and Chagossians’?

---

## 🇬🇧 UK Questions

1. What does the UK say it is trying to protect?

2. Is the UK framing the deal as:
   - legal-risk management,
   - decolonisation,
   - base continuity,
   - cost control,
   - China management,
   - or US relationship maintenance?

3. What colonial history is being softened or omitted?

4. What domestic political pressure is shaping the UK line?

5. Which UK politicians, think tanks, lobbyists, or commentators are briefing against or for the deal?

6. Do those actors have defence, AI, space, nuclear, contractor, legal, media, or Indo-Pacific interests?

7. Are we distinguishing UK public interest from UK contractor interest?

8. Are we distinguishing national security from political embarrassment?

---

## 🇺🇸 US Questions

1. What does the US need Diego Garcia for?

2. Is the US objection, support, or caution specific, or just wrapped in broad “security” language?

3. What operational categories are publicly acknowledged:
   - logistics,
   - airfield use,
   - naval support,
   - communications,
   - intelligence,
   - space surveillance,
   - telecoms,
   - or data infrastructure?

4. What categories are not disclosed, and why?

5. How does US approval or non-approval affect UK policy?

6. What role does US domestic politics play?

7. Are US defence-access pressures affecting how journalists can report the wider defence bridge?

8. Are US contractor interests being treated as identical to US national interest?

---

## 🌊 India And Indian Ocean Questions

1. Is India being treated as a background actor, or as a regional power with direct Indian Ocean interests?

2. Does the article explain India’s relationship with Mauritius?

3. Does it explain how India can support Mauritian sovereignty while also supporting continued US presence against China?

4. Does the article distinguish India’s structural interests from the current Modi government’s political style?

5. Does it explain India’s interest in:
   - maritime security,
   - China management,
   - Mauritius partnership,
   - strategic autonomy,
   - ASEAN,
   - defence technology,
   - and avoiding an Anglosphere-only Indo-Pacific architecture?

6. Does the piece ask what kind of Indian Ocean order India wants around Diego Garcia?

7. Does it explain why AUKUS may make the US–UK bridge more complicated from India’s point of view?

8. Does it treat smaller Indian Ocean states as political actors, or as scenery?

---

## 🐉 China Questions

1. Is China being invoked as a specific risk or as a general pressure word?

2. What exact China risk is claimed?

3. Who is making the claim?

4. What evidence supports it?

5. Does the treaty increase, decrease, or manage that risk?

6. Why would Mauritian sovereignty necessarily increase China risk?

7. What role does India play in mitigating or reframing China risk?

8. Who benefits if “China” becomes the dominant frame?

9. Is “China” being used to avoid discussing Chagossians, Mauritius, contractors, or legal history?

---

## 🇦🇺 AUKUS And Indo-Pacific Questions

1. Is AUKUS relevant to the framing, or being smuggled in without explanation?

2. Does the story distinguish US–UK defence continuity from US–UK–Australia defence integration?

3. Does it explain how AUKUS changes the regional reading of undersea, nuclear, AI, autonomy, cable, and maritime-security infrastructure?

4. Does it ask whether India views AUKUS as useful, exclusionary, or both?

5. Does it ask how smaller Indian Ocean states experience Anglosphere defence architecture?

6. Is the article treating Indo-Pacific security as a regional question or only as an Anglosphere strategy?

---

## 🛡️ Contractor Questions

1. Which companies directly support base operations on Diego Garcia?

2. Which companies support construction, facilities, logistics, airfield operations, port operations, housing, utilities, fuel, telecoms, environmental services, or security?

3. Which contractors are incumbents, challengers, joint-venture partners, or subcontractors?

4. Are procurement disputes, protests, or contract transitions relevant?

5. Which contractors have overlapping interests in defence, AI, space, cyber, telecoms, nuclear, public-sector outsourcing, or infrastructure?

6. Who owns or finances the visible contractors?

7. Which banks, private-equity firms, institutional investors, insurers, or parent companies sit behind them?

8. Which firms benefit from the base environment remaining predictable?

9. Which firms might gain from a changed sovereignty wrapper?

10. Are contractors being described as neutral service providers when they also have commercial preferences?

11. Are we distinguishing contractor continuity from national security?

---

## 🛰️ AI, Space, Telecoms, And Data Questions

1. What categories of space-related activity are publicly acknowledged at or around Diego Garcia?

2. Does the article distinguish space surveillance from rocket launch activity?

3. Are there satellite communications, telemetry, tracking, frequency, telecoms, or data-relay questions?

4. What AI-enabled systems might attach to base operations, logistics, maintenance, surveillance, maritime-domain awareness, environmental monitoring, or cyber security?

5. Which systems are military, commercial, dual-use, contractor-operated, or undisclosed?

6. Who oversees these systems?

7. Who receives assurances about dual-use activity?

8. What can be disclosed at category level without exposing operational details?

9. Are we avoiding the “secret rocket pad” trap while still asking real infrastructure questions?

10. Are we explaining that modern bases are data environments, not just physical sites?

---

## ⚖️ Jurisdiction Questions

1. What legal wrapper currently governs the base?

2. What legal wrapper would govern the base under Mauritian sovereignty?

3. What changes for:
   - civil jurisdiction,
   - criminal jurisdiction,
   - contractor liability,
   - labour rights,
   - immigration,
   - customs,
   - environmental duties,
   - telecoms and spectrum,
   - procurement,
   - policing,
   - dispute resolution,
   - and access?

4. Who can inspect?

5. Who can sue?

6. Who can ask questions?

7. Who can object?

8. Who receives assurances?

9. Which actors benefit if the old wrapper remains intact?

10. Which actors benefit if the wrapper changes?

---

## 📰 Media Pressure Questions

1. Is the BBC or another outlet reporting inside a prior pressure field?

2. Has the outlet previously faced state, regulatory, legal, access, or reputational pressure from actors connected to this story?

3. Could pressure on a foreign bureau create caution in UK output?

4. Are local staff, fixers, translators, sources, or partner organisations at risk if coverage is too direct?

5. Is US defence-access pressure relevant to reporting on a US–UK defence bridge?

6. Is India-facing regulatory memory relevant?

7. Are UK public-service broadcasting pressures relevant?

8. Are contractors, think tanks, diaspora groups, political offices, or public-affairs firms briefing aggressively?

9. Are search, social, or platform dynamics pushing one frame repeatedly?

10. Are journalists being nudged toward:
   - only China,
   - only Labour weakness,
   - only national security,
   - only decolonisation,
   - only cost,
   - only base continuity,
   - or only anti-imperial symbolism?

11. Does the piece explain the pressure field, or simply reproduce it?

---

## 🏛️ Legitimacy Questions

1. Which universities, think tanks, institutes, expert panels, or policy forums are being cited?

2. What funding, partnerships, defence links, AI links, contractor links, donor relationships, or government relationships do they have?

3. Are experts being used to clarify the issue or to launder a preferred frame?

4. Are colonial harms being made technical?

5. Are contractor interests being made respectable through institutional language?

6. Are voices from Mauritius, Chagossian communities, Indian Ocean states, and independent experts included?

7. Is Oxford-style or elite-institution legitimacy being treated as neutral?

8. Who gets to sound serious?

9. Who gets framed as emotional, activist, naïve, or inconvenient?

---

## 🧯 Evidence Discipline Questions

1. Is this claim evidenced, inferred, or speculative?

2. Have we labelled inference as inference?

3. Are we naming a company only where there is a documented link?

4. Are we separating direct Chagos links from broader bridge-sector examples?

5. Are we distinguishing space relevance from launch activity?

6. Are we distinguishing AI-enabled infrastructure from a secret AI programme?

7. Are we distinguishing media pressure from media capture?

8. Are we distinguishing India’s structural interests from the current Modi government’s style?

9. Are we distinguishing Mauritius from Chagossians?

10. Are we distinguishing China risk from China panic?

11. Are we distinguishing national security from contractor convenience?

12. Are we asking for category-level transparency where operational detail cannot be public?

13. Would this claim survive hostile scrutiny?

14. If not, should it be sharpened, caveated, or dropped?

---

## 🛠️ Minimal Democratic Briefing Questions

At minimum, a serious public briefing should answer:

1. What is Chagos?

2. What is Diego Garcia?

3. Who are Chagossians, and what rights or remedies are at issue?

4. What does Mauritius gain?

5. What do the UK and US retain?

6. What does India want around the Indian Ocean frame?

7. What specific China risks are being claimed?

8. Which contractors support the base environment?

9. What categories of AI, space, telecoms, cyber, logistics, and data activity are acknowledged?

10. What categories are explicitly not happening?

11. What is withheld from disclosure, and on what grounds?

12. Who oversees the arrangement?

13. Who audits contractors?

14. Who receives environmental assurances?

15. Who can reopen the settlement later?

16. Who benefits from public confusion?

---

## 🧠 Core Reading

Good Chagos journalism should not pick one frame and delete the rest.

It should keep the stack visible.

The base is real.

The sovereignty claim is real.

The displacement is real.

The India layer is real.

The contractor layer is real.

The AI, space, telecoms, and data layer is real.

The media-pressure layer is real.

The China concern may be real, but it is not the whole story.

The job is not to prove a secret.

The job is to stop public explanation being chopped into pieces small enough for powerful actors to hide behind.
