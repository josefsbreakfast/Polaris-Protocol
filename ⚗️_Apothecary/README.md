# ⚗️ Apothecary  
**First created:** 2026-05-14 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-14  

*An ecological framework for understanding digital behaviour, informational atmospheres, algorithmic constitutions, and the maintenance of cognitive sovereignty in networked environments.*  

---

> “The poison and the medicine are often separated only by dosage, context, and repetition.”  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

**Apothecary** approaches the internet as a pharmacological environment:  
a place of stimulants, sedatives, intoxicants, compulsions, atmospheres, habits, and adaptive constitutions.  

Rather than treating digital behaviour purely as a matter of morality, intelligence, or ideology, this framework examines how platforms interact with:

- temperament,
- emotional regulation,
- identity formation,
- nervous system states,
- attention economies,
- social reinforcement,
- and environmental conditioning.  

The cluster borrows loosely from historical humoural and constitutional models — not as literal medicine, but as symbolic language for recurring behavioural tendencies visible in contemporary online life.  

In this framework:

- **Constitutions** describe baseline tendencies,
- **Temperaments** describe recurring behavioural expressions,
- **Distempers** describe dysregulated or pathological states,
- **Tonics** describe restorative practices,
- **Remedies** describe practical interventions,
- and **Vapours** describe the wider emotional climates generated by networked systems.  

The aim is diagnostic and ecological rather than accusatory.  
The framework asks:

> *What kinds of digital environments produce which states of mind?*  

And:

> *What forms of practice help preserve agency, continuity, discernment, and psychological resilience inside algorithmic systems optimised for capture?*

# 🌡️ Constitutions  

Constitutions describe enduring tendencies in how individuals relate to information, stimulation, conflict, novelty, and attention.  

These are not rigid personality categories, but recurring orientations which platforms often amplify through recommendation systems, emotional reinforcement, and identity signalling dynamics.

- 🕳️ Conspiracy Attraction

# 🫙 Distempers  

Distempers describe dysregulated states where informational environments begin to impair judgment, emotional regulation, social trust, or behavioural autonomy.  

The distinction between constitution and distemper is important:

> a tendency becomes a distemper when it loses proportionality, flexibility, or grounding.  

Distempers are therefore understood as ecological overload states rather than purely individual moral failures.

# 🪴 Tonics  

Tonics are restorative practices which strengthen continuity of attention, embodied grounding, reflective capacity, and resistance to compulsive algorithmic capture.  

Unlike remedies, tonics are usually slow-building practices rather than emergency interventions.  

### Nodes  

- 🌲 Offline Grounding  
- 📖 Longform Reading  
- 🕯️ Delayed Response Rituals  
- 🎨 Private Creativity  
- 🚶 Embodied Routines  
- ☕️ Slow Internet Practices  
- 🌤️ Attention Rebalancing  

---

# 💊 Remedies  

Remedies are practical countermeasures designed to interrupt harmful feedback loops and restore intentionality within digital environments.  

These interventions focus on friction, pacing, context restoration, and reducing passive capture.  

### Nodes  

- 🔕 Notification Reduction  
- ⏳ Friction And Pause Systems  
- 📵 Feed Interruption Methods  
- 🧹 Algorithmic Hygiene  
- 🪟 Context Reintroduction  
- 🧭 Intentional Navigation  

---

# 🌫️ Vapours  

Vapours describe large-scale informational atmospheres: diffuse emotional climates generated collectively through platforms, media ecosystems, algorithmic amplification, and memetic circulation.  

Unlike individual constitutions or distempers, vapours function environmentally.  

People do not merely produce them — they also breathe them.  

### Nodes  

- 🌩️ Outrage Atmospheres  
- 👁️ Conspiracy Haze  
- 🎪 Meme Fever  
- 🕸️ Post-Truth Fog  
- 📉 Collapse Moods  
- 📺 Spectacle Weather  
- 🪞 Identity Climate

# ✨ Key Themes  

## 🧠 Attention As Governance  

Attention is treated not merely as a cognitive resource, but as a governable infrastructure shaped by incentives, friction, emotional intensity, and platform architecture.  

---

## 🌡️ Algorithmic Ecology  

Platforms are analysed as environments which selectively reward certain emotional and behavioural states.  

The system is therefore understood ecologically rather than purely individually.  

---

## 🪞 Identity As Feedback Loop  

Online identities are often shaped recursively through performance, surveillance, audience expectation, and algorithmic reinforcement.  

This creates environments where authenticity, performance, irony, and branding increasingly blur together.  

---

## 🧴 Hygiene Over Purity  

The framework prioritises maintenance, discernment, pacing, and recovery over fantasies of complete informational purity or total disengagement.  

The question is not:

> “How do we escape the network entirely?”  

But rather:

> “How do we remain psychologically coherent within it?”
