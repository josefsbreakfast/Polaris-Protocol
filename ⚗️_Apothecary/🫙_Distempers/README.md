# 🫙 Distempers  
**First created:** 2026-05-12 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-14  

*An examination of dysregulated informational states, maladaptive engagement loops, and the interaction between human vulnerabilities and algorithmically amplified behavioural environments.*  

---

> “A constitution becomes a distemper when it loses proportionality, flexibility, or grounding.”  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

The **Distempers** cluster examines maladaptive or destabilised informational states produced through prolonged interaction between:

- nervous systems,
- digital environments,
- emotional reinforcement systems,
- algorithmic amplification,
- and social contagion dynamics.  

Unlike **Constitutions**, which describe enduring tendencies, distempers describe states in which behavioural or emotional patterns become:

- compulsive,
- disproportionate,
- self-reinforcing,
- psychologically exhausting,
- or increasingly detached from grounding conditions.  

The framework intentionally avoids treating these states purely as:

- moral failures,
- personal weakness,
- or isolated pathology.  

Instead, distempers are approached ecologically:

> as interactions between predispositions and environments optimised for capture, escalation, repetition, and emotional activation.  

Different constitutions tend toward different distempers under prolonged stress, saturation, or destabilising informational conditions.  

---

## 🌡️ Core Principles  

### 🧠 Dysregulation Is Often Environmental  

Many harmful online states are intensified not simply by individual traits, but by systems which continuously reward:

- urgency,
- outrage,
- novelty,
- vigilance,
- visibility,
- and compulsive repetition.  

Distempers therefore frequently emerge through sustained environmental exposure rather than isolated personal failure.  

---

### 🔁 Reinforcement Creates Entrenchment  

Algorithmic systems often strengthen dysregulated states by rewarding the behaviours associated with them through:

- visibility,
- engagement,
- emotional stimulation,
- social reinforcement,
- and recommendation loops.  

The behaviour becomes self-reinforcing both psychologically and structurally.  

---

### 🌫️ Distempers Often Feel Rational From Inside  

Many dysregulated states maintain themselves because they provide:

- coherence,
- stimulation,
- certainty,
- identity,
- urgency,
- or emotional regulation.  

As a result, participants may experience the behaviour not as dysfunction, but as necessity or adaptation.  

---

### 🧴 Recovery Requires More Than Self-Blame  

Because distempers emerge environmentally as well as psychologically, meaningful recovery often requires:

- environmental modification,
- pacing,
- friction,
- grounding,
- relational support,
- and interruption of reinforcement loops.  

---

## 🪞 Escalation Dynamics  

Distempers frequently intensify through recursive interaction between:

- platform architecture,
- emotional reinforcement,
- social visibility,
- and ambient informational atmospheres.  

Examples include:

- outrage atmospheres intensifying choleric escalation,
- conspiracy haze deepening melancholic paranoia loops,
- meme fever accelerating sanguine overstimulation,
- or passive recommendation systems reinforcing phlegmatic dissociation.  

The same environmental conditions can therefore produce different forms of dysregulation depending upon constitutional tendencies.  

---

## 📡 Behavioural Capture  

Many distempers become difficult to interrupt because platforms convert dysregulated engagement into:

- retention,
- visibility,
- emotional intensity,
- and behavioural predictability.  

This creates situations where:

> the system benefits from the continuation of the destabilised state.  

The framework therefore treats distempers not only as psychological phenomena, but also as infrastructural ones.  

---

## ✨ Key Themes  

### 🧠 Distempers Are Not Entire Identities  

The framework deliberately avoids reducing individuals to their dysregulated states.  

Distempers are treated as:

- conditions,
- patterns,
- loops,
- or ecological states,  

not fixed moral essences.  

---

### 🌡️ Emotional Intensity Distorts Proportionality  

Many distempers narrow perception toward:

- immediacy,
- certainty,
- urgency,
- and emotionally reinforcing interpretations.  

Over time this can reduce:

- ambiguity tolerance,
- contextual thinking,
- and reflective distance.  

---

### 🪞 Online Systems Industrialise Vulnerability  

Modern platforms often scale and monetise:

- outrage,
- insecurity,
- paranoia,
- compulsive checking,
- performativity,
- and passive dissociation.  

Distempers therefore cannot be fully understood outside platform economics and attention systems.  

---

### 🌱 Recovery Requires Re-Grounding  

Effective recovery usually depends upon restoring:

- embodiment,
- pacing,
- social continuity,
- emotional regulation,
- environmental friction,
- and attentional boundaries.  

The goal is not total purity, but renewed proportionality and agency.
