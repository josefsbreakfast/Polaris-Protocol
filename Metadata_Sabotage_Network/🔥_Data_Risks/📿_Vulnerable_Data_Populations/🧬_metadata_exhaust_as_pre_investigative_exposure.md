### 1. What â€œmetadata exhaustâ€ actually is  

Metadata exhaust refers to the passive data traces generated by everyday activity, including:

- search queries and browsing patterns  
- app installation and usage changes  
- location pings and mobility shifts  
- call and messaging frequency changes  
- device-level telemetry  
- time-of-day behavioural anomalies

This data is:
- routinely collected  
- widely traded  
- legally purchased  
- aggregated at scale  
- analysed probabilistically  

It does not require content access to be revealing.

### 2. Why sexual offence reporting creates a distinct metadata signature  

In the period surrounding a rape or sexual assault, victims often show:

- sudden searches related to reporting, consent, or trauma  
- visits to NHS, SARC, or counselling websites  
- location changes near police stations or clinics  
- spikes in helpline or support app use  
- disrupted sleep and mobility patterns  
- increased legal or advocacy site traffic  

Individually, these signals mean little.  
Together, they form a **recognisable behavioural cluster**.

This cluster can be identified algorithmically without knowing who the person is â€” at first.

---

### 3. From probabilistic signal to identifiable person  

Once a behavioural cluster is detected, correlation becomes straightforward:

- location data narrows geography  
- device identifiers stabilise the subject  
- recurring routines reveal home/work zones  
- social graph proximity reduces ambiguity  
- force boundaries and service catchments infer jurisdiction  

At this stage, an actor may not know:
- the name  
- the allegation  
- the evidence  

But they can know:
- *someone has likely reported or is about to report rape*  
- *roughly where they live*  
- *which institutions they will interact with*  

This is sufficient for targeting.
