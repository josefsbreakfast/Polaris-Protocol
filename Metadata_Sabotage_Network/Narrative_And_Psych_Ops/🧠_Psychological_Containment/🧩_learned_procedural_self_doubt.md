### Gendered and marginalised amplification
Learned procedural self-doubt is disproportionately reinforced among:
- women
- marginalised groups
- survivors
- those without institutional backing

These individuals are more likely to be required to:
- prove competence repeatedly
- manage tone as well as content
- supply certainty without access to information
- remain calm under provocation

Doubt accumulates not because of incapacity, but because the cost of being misread is higher.

### Why reassurance alone does not resolve it
Learned procedural self-doubt is not resolved by affirmation or encouragement alone.

Because it was trained through repeated system interaction, it is undone through:
- consistent, accurate responses
- predictable rule application
- being taken seriously without self-erasure
- procedural closure rather than indefinite delay

Without these conditions, doubt persists even when the individual intellectually recognises that the system is at fault.
