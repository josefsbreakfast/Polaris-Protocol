### 1. SEO Infrastructure as an Attack Surface  

Search engines rely on:
- Crawlers
- Crawl budgets
- Index prioritisation
- Canonical and duplication signals

These systems can be **intentionally stressed or distorted** without breaching security perimeters.

Common pressure techniques include:
- Hostile or excessive crawling
- Parameter and URL explosion
- Inducing crawl inefficiency
- Forcing index bloat and duplication
- Triggering performance degradation visible to search engines

No content alteration is required.

### 2. Why This Is Targeted, Not Universal  

These techniques carry real costs:
- Bandwidth and infrastructure
- IP rotation and bot management
- Risk of fingerprinting or blocking

As a result, deployment is **selective**.

Targets are typically:
- Politically inconvenient actors
- Under-resourced civic groups
- Independent media
- Decentralised movements
- Organisations reliant on SEO but lacking deep technical capacity

### 4. Political and Lobby Incentives  

SEO-layer pressure is attractive to political or lobby actors because it:

- Suppresses voice without censorship optics  
- Avoids platform moderation pathways

- Produces deniability and attribution ambiguity

- Fragmenting indexing context
- Allowing rebuttals or hostile interpretations to outrank originals
- Dislocating arguments from their framing

This produces confusion rather than silence.
