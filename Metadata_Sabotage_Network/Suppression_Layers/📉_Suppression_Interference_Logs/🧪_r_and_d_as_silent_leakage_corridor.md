### 1. What â€œR&Dâ€ actually means in justice contexts

---

### 2. Why R&D is structurally leaky  

R&D environments are designed to:
- copy data  
- transform formats  
- sample subsets  
- generate logs  
- cache outputs  
- test edge cases  
- reproduce bugs  

This requires:
- staging environments  
- test datasets  
- developer access  
- contractor involvement  
- cloud infrastructure  

None of these environments natively enforce:
- offence-code sensitivity  
- victim-protection flags  
- justice-specific access constraints  

As a result, **classification collapses silently**.

---

### 3. The corridor effect  

R&D does not look like a single exit point.  
It behaves like a corridor:

- data enters under authorisation  
- fragments persist across systems  
- copies proliferate invisibly  
- context is progressively stripped  
- audit trails thin out  
- responsibility diffuses  

By the time exposure is noticed, it is:
- difficult to localise  
- hard to prove  
- legally ambiguous  
- organisationally deniable  

This is why it rarely triggers incident response.

### 4. Vendor R&D: lawful, opaque, and external  

When private vendors are involved:

- engineers may access live or mirrored data

- cloud services may transiently store content  
- subcontractors may be used without visibility

- permit data handling â€œfor service improvementâ€
