## 1. Why Newsrooms Deprioritise Volatile Stories

1. **Will this story destabilise institutional relationships?**  
2. **Can it be reported without legal exposure or political backlash?**

## 4. When Deprioritisation Fails  
Soft burial becomes untenable when:

- bodies enter hospital,  
- youth amplifies moral pressure,  
- public curiosity grows due to narrative gaps,  
- senior officials display inconsistent knowledge,  
- alternative media fills the vacuum.

In these cases, deprioritisation produces the opposite effect:  
the absence of coverage becomes a story in itself.

## 5. Democratic Legitimacy and Editorial Silence  
Deprioritisation is not neutral.  
It shapes what the public believes is happening and why.

When young pre-trial detainees experience life-threatening decline and newsrooms respond with euphemised, inconsistent coverage, the public experiences:

- interpretive instability,  
- distrust in institutions,  
- suspicion of political pressure,  
- a widening gap between lived perception and official narrative.

In high-alert environments, this gap becomes a democratic vulnerability.
