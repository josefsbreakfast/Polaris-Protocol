### 1. Predictability Without Resolution

- limited accountability,
- narrow blame,
- no systemic reckoning.

### 2. The Emotional Economics of Delay  
Dragging things out serves a psychological function:
- hope keeps people compliant,
- uncertainty keeps attention focused,
- disappointment is easier to absorb when it arrives in increments.

A sudden refusal would provoke outrage.  
A prolonged let-down produces fatigue.

Fatigue is easier to govern.

### 5. Why Survivors Pay the Highest Cost  
Survivors are uniquely harmed by prolonged uncertainty:
- trauma remains open-ended,
- closure is indefinitely deferred,
- emotional boundaries cannot be rebuilt.

The system externalises the cost of delay onto those already harmed.

This is not care.  
It is extraction of resilience.

### 6. The Benefit to Power  
For institutions and implicated elites, drawn-out let-down offers:
- time to manage reputation,
- time to coordinate defence,
- time for public interest to thin,
- time for moral clarity to dull.

By the time nothing happens, fewer people are watching.
