### Patterned Silence  

Absence becomes diagnostic when it is:
- repeated across cases,
- aligned with specific actors or topics,
- time-bounded to moments of controversy or transition,
- present in one population and absent in another.

A single blank record proves nothing.  
A **pattern of blanks** is a signal.

To read absence, establish:
1. What records *do* exist nearby?
2. What processes imply the missing artefact should exist?
3. Where does the silence begin and end?

Absence is legible only in relation to **contextual density**.
