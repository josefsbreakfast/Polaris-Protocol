### 1. Ambiguity tolerance as a risk variable  

Institutions vary widely in how much uncertainty they can tolerate.

Low ambiguity tolerance often correlates with:
- stricter regulators  
- tighter audit cycles  
- conservative legal cultures  
- higher reputational sensitivity  

For these actors, ambiguity itself is dangerous.

---

### 2. Why the first mover is rarely the worst actor  

The first mover is often:
- least involved in the original decisions  
- least exposed substantively  
- most exposed reputationally  
- most constrained procedurally  

This combination makes silence costlier than disclosure.

---

### 3. The logic of moving first  

First movers calculate that:
- speaking early allows framing control  
- clarity reduces associative risk  
- delay increases vulnerability  
- transparency limits future exposure  

Breaking silence is therefore a **defensive optimisation**, not a confession.

---

### 4. System effects of first-mover action  

Once one actor clarifies their position:
- continued silence by others appears evasive  
- ambiguity becomes unevenly distributed  
- pressure propagates through the network  

The equilibrium shifts irreversibly.
