### 1. What regulatory adjacency means  

Institutions do not operate in isolation.  
When regulators engage with one part of a sector, **risk posture shifts across the whole network**.

This creates:
- heightened compliance awareness  
- accelerated decision-making  
- increased sensitivity to timelines  

---

### 2. Why delay becomes dangerous  

During periods of active oversight:
- delay attracts attention  
- inertia appears negligent  
- procedural gaps become conspicuous  

Institutions therefore act earlier than they otherwise would.

---

### 3. Adjacency without causality  

Regulatory adjacency does **not** imply:
- direct investigation  
- targeted scrutiny  
- causal linkage  

It describes **environmental pressure**, not intent.
