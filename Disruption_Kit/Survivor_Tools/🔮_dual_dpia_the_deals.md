## ğŸ› ï¸ Systematic Use (Repeatable Method)

### Phase 1: Contract Mapping
For each vendor:
- list departments / authorities  
- list stated purposes  
- note affected populations  

You are mapping **processing contexts**, not organisations.

---

### Phase 2: Controller FOI (Governance Layer)

**Request focus:**
- DPIAs relating to the deployment or use of the vendor/system  
- risk registers, ethics approvals, sign-off records  
- procurement documents referencing data protection, profiling, or inference  

**Key technique:**  
Always ask for *existence, dates, authors, and titles* even if content may be exempt.

---

### Phase 3: Controller SAR (Reality Layer, if applicable)

If you plausibly fall within scope:
- request personal data  
- inferred or derived attributes  
- profiles, flags, risk indicators  
- metadata relating to decision-making  

This tests whether DPIA claims match lived processing.

---

### Phase 4: Processor Request (Mirror Channel)

Depending on jurisdiction and status:
- SAR to the vendor (for your data)  
- transparency request asking what DPIA or risk materials they supplied  
- request for system documentation describing risks and mitigations  

You are not asking them to *own* the DPIAâ€”only to describe their contribution.

---

### Phase 5: Comparison Pass

You are looking for:
- contradictions  
- omissions  
- scope mismatches  
- timeline inconsistencies  

Patterns across **multiple contracts** matter more than any single failure.
