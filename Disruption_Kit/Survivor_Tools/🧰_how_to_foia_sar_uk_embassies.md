Beyond passports and visas, embassies may process personal data relating to you if:

### 2. **You were mentioned in diplomatic or administrative correspondence**

- emails,
- briefings,

### 3. **Information was shared with or from UK authorities**
Embassies exchange information with:
- police,
- Home Office units,

- local authorities,

### 4. **Risk, security, or intelligence-adjacent material was generated**
This is poorly understood but lawful.

Embassies may:
- record observations,
- log concerns,
- receive intelligence reporting,
- assess reputational, security, or safeguarding implications involving individuals.

The existence of such data **does not imply criminality or wrongdoing**.  
Often it reflects precaution, context-gathering, or third-party reporting.

You may never have been notified.

## âš–ï¸ Your Information Rights (Two Different Tools)

### ğŸ” Freedom of Information Act (FOIA)

FOIA is used to request **information about systems and processes**, not personal files.

FOIA can be used to request:
- policies and guidance,
- safeguarding frameworks,
- training materials,
- contracts and contractors,
- aggregate statistics,
- decision-making processes.

**Important points:**
- FOIA applies to the FCDO, including overseas posts.
- Requests are made centrally, not to individual embassies.
- International relations and national security exemptions are common.
- Requests for *your own data* are usually redirected to SAR.

FOIA asks:  
**â€œWhat does the system do?â€**

### ğŸ‘¤ Subject Access Request (SAR)

A SAR is usually the **stronger tool** if you think data *about you* exists.

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:
- know whether your personal data is held,
- receive a copy of that data (with lawful redactions),
- understand why and how it was processed,
- know who it was shared with.

Key points:
- You SAR the **FCDO**, not an embassy as a separate entity.
- Data processed by embassy staff on FCDO systems is in scope.
- You do **not** need to know what exists to request it.
- You do **not** need to allege wrongdoing.

SARs are handled via the FCDOâ€™s **Data Protection Officer** channel, which is deliberately centralised and role-based to ensure compliance and auditability.

## ğŸ§­ Choosing the Right Route  

Use **FOIA** if you want:
- policy context,
- proof of institutional practice,
- to understand how safeguarding or intelligence processes work in general.

Use a **SAR** if you want:
- records that mention you,
- notes, referrals, or assessments about you,
- to understand how you were characterised or discussed.

They can be run **in parallel**, but SARs are often less negotiable.
