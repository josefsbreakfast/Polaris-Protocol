### 3. Golus Nationalism  

Golus (diasporic) nationalism reframes dispersion not as exile awaiting correction, but as a **political condition with its own ethics**.

Key features include:
- local embeddedness without assimilation  
- translocal solidarity without central authority  
- survival without supremacy  

Golus nationalism challenges the idea that safety requires dominance.

### 5. Anti-, Post-, and Non-Zionism  

These positions are often collapsed into hostility, but they reflect distinct relationships to history:

The problem is not disagreement.
