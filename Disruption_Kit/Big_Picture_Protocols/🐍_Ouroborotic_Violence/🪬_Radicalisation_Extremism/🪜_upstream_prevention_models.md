### 1. Community Cohesion and Cross-Group Contact

Strong evidence shows that sustained, meaningful contact across groups:

* reduces fear and stereotyping
* increases empathy and social trust
* weakens us-versus-them narratives

Effective models include:

* shared civic projects
* mixed-group youth programmes
* arts, sports, and cultural infrastructure
* local decision-making forums

Cohesion is not messaging; it is **shared experience**.

---

### 2. Youth Opportunity and Pathways

Youth vulnerability rises sharply when futures feel blocked.

Protective interventions include:

* apprenticeships and paid training
* clear routes from education to employment
* mentoring and sponsorship
* recognition of informal skills

Where young people can imagine a future, extremist narratives lose urgency.

---

### 3. Anti-Discrimination Enforcement

Everyday discrimination corrodes belonging.

Evidence-based prevention requires:

* robust enforcement of equality law
* accountability for discriminatory policing or hiring
* accessible reporting mechanisms
* visible consequences for institutional bias

Fair treatment is not symbolic â€” it is preventative.

---

### 4. Mental-Health and Trauma Support

Many vulnerable individuals carry:

* unaddressed trauma
* depression or anxiety
* identity-related stress
* intergenerational harm

Accessible, culturally competent mental-health care:

* reduces isolation
* interrupts grievance escalation
* restores self-worth

Care is a security intervention.

---

### 5. Safe Political Expression

Extremist movements thrive where legitimate dissent feels impossible.

Upstream models protect:

* freedom to criticise government
* space for political anger without punishment
* non-violent routes to influence

Democracy itself is a protective factor.
