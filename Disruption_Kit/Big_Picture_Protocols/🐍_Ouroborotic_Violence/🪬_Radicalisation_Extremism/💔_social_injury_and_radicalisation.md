### 2. Humiliation as a Radicalising Force

Humiliation differs from anger or grievance.

It involves:

* loss of status
* perceived disrespect
* inability to restore dignity through legitimate means

Humiliation creates a psychological demand for **moral repair**. Extremist movements promise that repair through narratives of honour, sacrifice, and restored meaning.

---

### 3. Blocked Life Paths

Radicalisation risk increases where:

* education does not translate into opportunity
* labour markets exclude by race or postcode
* adulthood milestones are unreachable
* futures feel foreclosed

When legitimate paths to purpose are blocked, illegitimate ones become visible.

* recognition and respect
* stable employment pathways
* civic participation

Where dignity is secure, extremist narratives lose traction.
