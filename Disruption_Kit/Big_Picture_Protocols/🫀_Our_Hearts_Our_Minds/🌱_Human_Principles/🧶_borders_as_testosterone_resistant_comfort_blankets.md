### 3. Patrol Comfort vs Continuity Comfort  

**Patrol-oriented comfort**
- Safety through defended boundaries  
- Identity anchored to territory  
- Threat imagined as invasion  
- Stability imagined as control  

**Continuity-oriented comfort**
- Safety through livability and access  
- Identity anchored to relationships and routines  
- Threat experienced as disruption, precarity, or harm  
- Stability imagined as care and continuity  

Borders overwhelmingly privilege the first mode.

This produces governance failure modes where:
- territory is defended while interiors rot  
- symbolic strength replaces material safety  
- escalation is treated as reassurance
