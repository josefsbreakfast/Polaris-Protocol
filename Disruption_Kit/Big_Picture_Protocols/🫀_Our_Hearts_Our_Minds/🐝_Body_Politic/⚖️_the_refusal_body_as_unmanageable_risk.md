## 4. Why Institutions Cannot Stabilise Refusal  
Refusal bodies disrupt four internal assumptions:

### **a. The Assumption of Benevolent Containment**  
Institutions assume they can maintain safety.  
When a person deteriorates by choice, containment appears ineffective.

### **b. The Assumption of Identifiable Risk**  
Risk frameworks classify harm as outward (public safety) or inward (self-harm).  
Hunger strikes belong to neither category â€” they are communicative refusal.  
The system lacks a field for that.

### **c. The Assumption of Linear Time**  
Institutions rely on procedural timelines: hearings, reviews, medical checks.  
The hunger strike accelerates biological time beyond administrative pace.

### **d. The Assumption of State Interpretive Control**  
Institutions assume they can set the narrative.  
The refusal body creates a counter-narrative through embodiment.

Refusal is unmanageable because it breaks the epistemic foundation of governance, not because it threatens violence.
