### 1. A claim without coordinates  
â€œOver-diagnosisâ€ is presented as if it were self-evident. It is not.

In serious medical or statistical contexts, such a claim would require:

- diagnostic criteria under review

### 2. Over-diagnosed compared to *what*?  
There are several distinct phenomena routinely collapsed into one accusation:

- Increased recognition of previously underdiagnosed groups  
- Expanded diagnostic criteria reflecting better evidence  
- Post-crisis prevalence changes (e.g. post-COVID effects)  
- Increased access to assessment  
- Reduced stigma leading to help-seeking  

None of these are synonymous with over-diagnosis.

Failure to disaggregate them is not a minor error. It is the entire mechanism by which the rhetoric functions.

### 6. Who bears the burden of proof?

- disabled people must prove they are *not* over-diagnosed  
- clinicians must defend ordinary practice  
- patients must justify access to care  

This inversion is not accidental. It is how withdrawal of care is normalised without openly arguing for it.
