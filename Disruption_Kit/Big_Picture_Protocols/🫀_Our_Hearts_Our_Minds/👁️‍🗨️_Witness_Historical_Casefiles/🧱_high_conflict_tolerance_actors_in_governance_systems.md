### 2. Why institutions misread these actors  

Most governance systems assume:
- conflict avoidance as a default  
- escalation as emotionally driven  
- withdrawal under sustained friction  

High-conflict-tolerance actors violate all three assumptions.

As a result, institutions lack an interpretive framework for them.

---

### 3. Escalation without volatility  

These actors escalate:
- early  
- clearly  
- lawfully  
- proportionately  

They do not rely on emotional leverage.  
They rely on **procedural correctness**.

This creates confusion in systems that equate escalation with distress.

Governance processes often implicitly rely on:
- delay  
- opacity  
- hierarchy  
- resource asymmetry  

High-conflict-tolerance actors are largely immune to these pressures, rendering such tactics ineffective.

This immunity is frequently misinterpreted as defiance.
