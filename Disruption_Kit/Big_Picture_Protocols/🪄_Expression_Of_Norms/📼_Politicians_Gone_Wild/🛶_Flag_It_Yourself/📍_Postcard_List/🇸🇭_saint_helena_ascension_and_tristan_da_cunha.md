## First Postcard: Where Actually Is It?

Saint Helena, Ascension Island and Tristan da Cunha are in the South Atlantic.

They are not close to each other in any normal human sense.

They are linked constitutionally, but that does not mean they function like three stops on a convenient bus route.

A serious postcard must notice:

- Saint Helena has a settled island community and is historically associated with Napoleon’s exile;
- Ascension Island has a distinctive military, airfield, communications, and conservation role;
- Tristan da Cunha is one of the world’s most remote inhabited islands;
- the distances between them are material;
- transport is not casual;
- emergency planning is not decorative;
- and “remote” is not a personality trait.

Contestants may not say:

> “We’ll just do them together.”

No, Colin.

You will not “just” do anything in the South Atlantic.

## Three Places, Not One Convenient Dot

This territory is a useful punishment for map-brain because it looks singular from far away and becomes complicated as soon as you zoom in.

Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha are constitutionally linked, but their histories and present realities are very different.

A contestant who says:

> “Basically the same place.”

has failed before unpacking.

Saint Helena has settlement history, exile history, a local community, airport debates, endemic biodiversity, and a long relationship with isolation and supply.

Ascension has military and communications significance, an airfield, limited permanent local civic life in the ordinary sense, Green Mountain, turtles, seabirds, and questions about governance for a working island.

Tristan has a tiny resident community, extreme isolation, volcanic history, communal landholding, fishing, limited access, rockhopper penguins, and logistics that make “pop over” sound clinically unserious.

The flag emoji is doing too much work.

Give it a chair.

## How It Became An Overseas Territory

Saint Helena was discovered by Portuguese navigators in the early modern period and later became an English East India Company and British imperial staging point in the South Atlantic.

It became strategically useful as a stop on ocean routes.

It is also famously associated with Napoleon Bonaparte’s exile after Waterloo.

Ascension Island was garrisoned by Britain in the nineteenth century, partly in relation to Saint Helena and wider South Atlantic strategy.

Tristan da Cunha was also annexed and garrisoned by Britain in the nineteenth century, with settlement developing into one of the most remote communities in the world.

This is not one neat origin story.

It is sea routes, empire, exile, military utility, communications, isolation, local settlement, dependency, and the long admin afterlife of Britain needing dots in the ocean for practical reasons.

Contestants should notice that “strategic island” usually means:

- ships;
- cables;
- airfields;
- surveillance;
- exile;
- labour;
- supply;
- military planning;
- or someone else living with the consequences.

It rarely just means “nice place for a tiny flag.”

Ascension Island is often understood through infrastructure.

It has an airfield.

Contestants may discuss this.

## Tristan Is Not Remote Vibes

Tristan da Cunha is one of the most remote inhabited islands in the world.

Reuters recently described Tristan as having no air access, with only a few ships each year from Cape Town, and a population of roughly 216 people in 2026. (Reuters)

Contestants may find this charming.

They should be careful.

Remote does not mean:

- cute;
- simple;
- timeless;
- low-risk;
- self-sufficient in a fantasy way;
- or available as an aesthetic for other people’s adventure content.

Remote means:

- medical access is hard;
- evacuation is hard;
- supply is hard;
- weather matters;
- ships matter;
- communications matter;
- public health matters;
- outside support may be slow;
- and a minor planning failure can become a serious event.

Contestants may not say:

> “Sounds peaceful.”

It may be peaceful.

It is also logistically unforgiving.

The ocean is not a wellness retreat.

## What The Craic Is Now

Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha form a British Overseas Territory with three distinct constituent parts.

The 2009 constitutional arrangements gave Ascension and Tristan more equal constitutional status rather than treating them simply as dependencies of Saint Helena.

The Governor is responsible across the territory and works with the governments of Saint Helena, Ascension and Tristan da Cunha. The Governor’s Office describes its role as supporting good governance across the three territories and discharging the Governor’s constitutional responsibilities. (GOV.UK)

In ordinary terms:

- the territory is not independent;
- it is not part of the United Kingdom;
- each constituent part has its own local governance arrangements;
- the UK retains significant responsibilities;
- distance makes governance practical, not theoretical;
- emergency access can be difficult;
- and the three places should not be flattened into one South Atlantic administrative blob.

Contestants may not say:

> “One Governor, one place.”

No.

One Governor.

Three realities.

Try again.

## Recent Public Health And Logistics Lesson

Remote islands make public health logistics visible very quickly.

In 2026, a suspected hantavirus case linked to a cruise ship visit to Tristan da Cunha led to urgent UK support and a wider response involving contact tracing, medical isolation, and medical support across remote South Atlantic locations. Reuters described Tristan as only reachable by sea, with limited ship access from Cape Town. (Reuters)

The point here is not to sensationalise illness.

The point is to notice what remoteness means.

Public health response in a remote territory is not just:

> “Send help.”

It is:

- who can travel;
- how quickly;
- by ship or air;
- with what medical staff;
- with what equipment;
- where isolation happens;
- how local services are protected;
- how risk is communicated;
- and what happens when a small island has a big-world problem.

Contestants who like speeches about British reach should read the logistics note before speaking.

## Rockhopper Penguin Advisory, Again

Tristan gives us more rockhopper business.

They are yellow-crested, cliff-adjacent, ocean-hardened chaos birds with strong opinions about terrain and no interest in validating British administrative categories.

Contestants may admire them.

- request a duet;

Rockhopper penguins are not a mandate.

They are rockhopper penguins.

Show some respect.

## Saint Helena’s Endemics Are Not Small Because The Island Is Small

Saint Helena is known for extraordinary endemic biodiversity.

This includes plants, birds, and invertebrates that evolved in isolation.

Contestants should notice that extinction risk is not proportional to font size on a map.

A tiny island can hold species found nowhere else.

A small habitat loss can be globally permanent.

A careless visitor can do damage much larger than their passport stamp.

The map is not the habitat.
