# 🧭 Official Contestant Kit List  
SATIRE. SATIRE. SATIRE.  
For contestants attempting global maritime statecraft with one dinghy, one flask of tea, and the confidence of a man who has never arranged port access.

---

## Welcome To Kit Inspection

Contestants on Flag It Yourself are reminded that they will only be pushed off from Dover once.

After that, they are on the itinerary.

This means they should pack for the trip they have politically implied is simple.

No one is forcing them to go.

However, once they have volunteered to prove that Britain must personally retain, reclaim, defend, explain, or emotionally supervise every distant dot on the map, production will not accept complaints about distance, weather, fuel, customs, penguins, monkeys, paperwork, sea conditions, local governments, or the discovery that the world is quite large.

This is not a punishment.

This is logistics.

If you wanted the opinion licence, you should have brought socks.

---

## Standard Issue Kit

Each contestant receives:

- one inflatable dinghy;
- one life vest;
- one laminated map;
- one laminated list of British Overseas Territories;
- one compass;
- one GPS tracker;
- one packet of emergency calorie biscuits that taste like compressed Cold War shelving;
- one flask of tea;
- twelve tiny Union Jack cocktail flags, suitable for Jubilee cupcakes, royal wedding traybakes, village fêtes, school halls, and unsupervised sovereignty theatre;
- one clipboard;
- one pencil attached to string;
- one waterproof pouch labelled “Please Do Not Tweet During The Maritime Incident.”

Contestants may notice that this kit is inadequate.

That is because the argument is inadequate.

If you notice what is missing, congratulations: you have discovered logistics.

---

## Personal Packing Advisory

Contestants are strongly advised to bring:

- waterproof clothing;
- warm clothing;
- sun protection;
- spare socks;
- actual drinking water, despite the tea;
- basic first aid;
- seasickness tablets;
- spare batteries;
- emergency shelter;
- local entry documentation;
- proof of travel insurance;
- evidence of next of kin;
- several changes of underwear;
- and a written explanation of why they previously thought “just keep it” was enough detail.

Contestants may not claim that packing is “bureaucracy gone mad.”

Packing is what happens when consequences are expected to arrive.

---

## The Tea Clause

Each contestant receives one flask of tea because this is Britain.

Is tea a sensible primary hydration plan for a maritime expedition?

No.

Will many contestants have previously made speeches about resilience, self-reliance, personal responsibility, and the national spirit?

Almost certainly.

This is therefore considered educational.

Contestants may request water.

Production may respond:

> Interesting. So material conditions matter now?

---

## The Biscuit Clause

Emergency calorie biscuits are provided.

They taste like cardboard, dust, institutional resolve, and a Soviet storage cupboard.

Contestants may complain.

Complaints will be logged under:

> “Man Discovers That Survival Food Is Not A Grazing Board.”

The biscuits are not there to be enjoyable.

They are there to reveal whether the contestant understands the difference between an aesthetic opinion and a supply chain.

---

## Cocktail Flag Issue

Each contestant receives twelve tiny Union Jack cocktail flags.

These are the kind normally used for:

- Jubilee cupcakes;
- royal wedding traybakes;
- village fête sponge;
- school hall party food;
- suspiciously dry birthday cake;
- and symbolic acts of unserious sovereignty.

The flags are not optional.

Contestants who believe distant territories must remain British for reasons of destiny, vibes, inherited blazer energy, or “geostrategy” will be expected to plant them personally, where local permission allows.

Each flag placement must include:

- visible flag;
- visible contestant;
- visible exhaustion;
- GPS confirmation;
- timestamp;
- and a short explanation of what legal, logistical, diplomatic, military, ecological, and human problem has supposedly been solved by sticking cake furniture into something damp.

Contestants are reminded:

> A cocktail flag is not a treaty instrument.

It is cake furniture.

Please behave accordingly.

---

## Communications Discipline

No social media posting while underway.

No Truth Social.  
No Mastodon.  
No dramatic Telegraph column dictated from the dinghy.  
No livestream called “Sovereignty At Sea.”  
No podcast episode titled “Why The Sea Is Woke.”  
No posting through it.

The contestant may briefly activate the GPS tracker for check-in purposes.

They may not use the device to complain that the sea is large.

Telegram access may be granted only for:

- emergency production updates;
- biscuit accountability;
- confirmation that the contestant has not attempted to annex a buoy;
- and evidence that nobody has upset a penguin.

Contestants are reminded that rowing requires hands.

Hands cannot simultaneously operate a dinghy, wave a cocktail flag, write a column, and blame lawyers for the tide.

Production staff reserve the right to shout:

> Less tweeting, more rowing.

---

## No Luxury Transport

Contestants may not use:

- business class;
- private jets;
- yachts;
- naval escorts;
- friendly superpowers quietly doing the expensive bit;
- special advisers carrying the snacks;
- staffers “handling the details”;
- helicopters;
- diplomatic cars;
- or any vessel whose primary function is making the contestant feel like empire had better upholstery.

The contestant wanted global reach.

They may reach globally.

---

## No SAS Rescue Clause

No one is sending the SAS to rescue you.

First, this is satire.

Second, the Treasury will not expense that.

Third, it would look too much like a holiday for them, and we are not opening that procurement line.

Contestants who become stuck due to:

- poor planning;
- customs complications;
- weather;
- local refusal;
- biscuit mismanagement;
- monkey interference;
- penguin disapproval;
- loss of cocktail flag;
- or the dawning realisation that a map is not a mobility plan;

must contact production in the approved manner and wait quietly.

The Foreign Office is not your mum.

The special forces are not your gap year retrieval service.

---

## Customs And Local Authority Documents

Contestants must carry all relevant documents for every location they intend to visit.

This includes:

- passport;
- visas where required;
- permits;
- local entry permissions;
- environmental permissions;
- filming permissions;
- evidence that they understand customs;
- written acknowledgement that “but this used to be British” is not a valid travel document.

If a contestant is refused entry, that is not proof of decline.

It may be proof of sovereignty.

Please update your notes.

---

## Wildlife Safety Addendum

The kit does not include permission to annoy animals.

Contestants must not:

- feed the monkeys;
- salute the penguins;
- chase turtles;
- stand on coral;
- shout “for king and country” at seabirds;
- describe seals as “strategically aligned”;
- use emergency biscuits as wildlife diplomacy;
- or attempt to photograph protected species with a cocktail flag unless this can be done lawfully, respectfully, and without making the animal look embarrassed.

If wildlife steals, destroys, rejects, ignores, or sits on your flag, production will not intervene.

That is now part of the record.

---

## What Is Not Included

The kit does not include:

- naval supremacy;
- treaty literacy;
- port access;
- local consent;
- fuel planning;
- legal authority;
- constitutional clarity;
- historical absolution;
- military credibility;
- ecological permission;
- or an automatic right to have opinions about places you have annoyed.

These must be acquired separately.

Preferably before publication.

---

## Discovery Checklist

If, during the journey, the contestant asks:

- “Where do we refuel?”
- “Who maintains this?”
- “Who pays for this?”
- “Do we need permission?”
- “Why are the locals annoyed?”
- “Why is customs asking questions?”
- “Why is this so far away?”
- “Why has the monkey taken the flag?”
- “Why did nobody tell me the Antarctic was cold?”
- “Why is the sea doing that?”

then the contestant has successfully begun the learning process.

They may proceed to Module Two:

Maps Are Not Logistics.

---

## Production Warning

This node is satire.

It is not a proposal to endanger anyone.  
It is not a plan to place politicians in boats.  
It is not an incitement to harm, abandon, drown, harass, stalk, threaten, dox, or physically interfere with public figures.

The point is not that politicians should be sent to sea.

The point is that public claims about territory, borders, bases, maritime power, migration, empire, and sovereignty should survive contact with material reality before being treated as serious.

If your opinion requires other people to absorb distance, danger, hunger, law, weather, cost, paperwork, or humiliation while you remain warm and televised, your opinion is not ready.

Pack again.
