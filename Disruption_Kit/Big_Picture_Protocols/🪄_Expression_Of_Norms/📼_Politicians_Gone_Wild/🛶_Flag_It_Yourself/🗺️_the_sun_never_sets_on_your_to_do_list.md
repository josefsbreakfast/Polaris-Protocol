# 🗺️ The Sun Never Sets On Your To-Do List  
SATIRE. SATIRE. SATIRE.  
The official itinerary for contestants who believe Britain should retain every distant map-dot, strategic rock, former imperial node, damp memory object, and suspiciously pink historical possession.

---

## Welcome To The Itinerary

The United Kingdom currently has fourteen Overseas Territories.

This means contestants who believe Britain must retain every distant territory for reasons of destiny, vibes, blazer memory, naval ghosts, or “geostrategy” are advised to clear their diary.

This is not a weekend away.

This is not a city break.

This is not a National Trust walking loop with a tea room at the end.

This was the empire upon which the sun never set.

Why should it set on you?

Let’s get rowing.

---

## The Agreement

Nobody is forcing anyone to complete this itinerary.

Contestants are free to stay at home, enjoy a biscuit, look at the map from a safe distance, and reconsider whether they truly need to have public opinions about places they cannot locate without zooming out.

However, contestants who decline the itinerary must agree to the following:

> No more confident public statements about random bits of British territory, former British territory, disputed territory, overseas territory, strategic rocks, Commonwealth states, former colonies, islands, bases, penguin zones, monkey zones, or places they have only encountered as coloured shapes on a map.

This is not censorship.

This is quality control.

If you want the opinion licence, complete the route.

If you do not want to complete the route, please stop shouting “why don’t we just keep it?” from a warm room.

## Route Advisory

Contestants who previously used the phrase “just keep it” are reminded that “it” is not one place.

“It” may include:

- the Caribbean;
- the North Atlantic;
- the Mediterranean;
- Cyprus;
- the Indian Ocean;
- the South Atlantic;
- the Pacific;
- and the Antarctic.

This is not a small local rowing challenge.

This is a distributed imperial admin hangover with weather.

The contestant may not say “I meant strategically” after seeing the map.

The map is the strategy you asked for.

Proceed.

## Penalty Expansion Clause

Contestants begin with the Overseas Territories list.

However, the itinerary may expand.

Any contestant who complains about:

- the Commonwealth;
- former colonies;
- states that gained independence from Britain;
- countries that no longer accept British authority;
- governments that require visas, customs checks, immigration forms, local permits, or basic respect;
- anyone “forgetting their history” by not doing what Britain wants;

will have that location added to the route.

This is called the Penalty Expansion Clause.

The Foreign Office will not come and get you.

If you get stuck at customs because you attempted to enter a sovereign state while carrying twelve Union Jack cocktail flags, a damp laminated map, and a handwritten note saying “but empire,” that is part of the learning experience.

Contestants are reminded:

> Independence means they can say no.

This may be upsetting.

Please process those feelings while rowing.

## Local Consent Clause

Contestants must return with no unresolved complaints from the people who actually live in, near, govern, maintain, work on, remember, or were displaced from the place being discussed.

If production receives credible reports that the contestant has:

- annoyed residents;
- ignored local authority;
- treated people as scenery;
- attempted to plant a cocktail flag in an inappropriate location;
- explained their own sovereignty to someone else’s country;
- described local consent as “a complication”;
- used the phrase “but historically” within earshot of customs;
- caused anyone to say, “Who sent this damp man with cake decorations?”;

the contestant forfeits the right to continue making confident public claims about that territory.

This is called the Local Consent Clause.

The lesson is simple:

> If the people there are fucked off, your map is not finished.

A successful visit requires more than GPS proof and a flag.

It requires evidence that the contestant has not converted a disputed territory, overseas territory, former colony, independent state, local community, or maritime logistics problem into a personal heritage tantrum.

## Wildlife Non-Interference Clause

Contestants must not annoy the wildlife.

This includes, but is not limited to:

- penguins;
- seals;
- turtles;
- seabirds;
- monkeys;
- crabs;
- coral;
- nesting things;
- protected plants;
- creatures minding their own business near a beach;
- any animal that has not consented to appear in sovereignty content.

Specific warnings:

- Do not upset the penguins near the Antarctic or Falklands.
- Do not irritate the Gibraltar monkeys.
- Do not bribe wildlife with emergency biscuits.
- Do not ask a penguin whether it recognises British sovereignty.
- Do not declare a seabird “basically pro-British” because it stood near a flag.
- Do not plant a cocktail flag in coral.
- Do not attempt to annex a turtle.

Wildlife complaints count.

If a monkey steals your flag, that is between you and the monkey.

Production will not intervene.

## Crown Naturalist Obligation

If contestants are completing the route “for king and country,” they must also document the living world they encounter.

At each stop, contestants must provide:

- photographs of local wildlife;
- photographs of plant life and habitat;
- notes on protected areas, conservation rules, and local environmental sensitivities;
- confirmation that no animals, birds, monkeys, penguins, turtles, seals, crabs, coral, plants, or nesting sites were annoyed, handled, chased, fed, insulted, stood on, annexed, or forced to pose with a cocktail flag;
- evidence that the contestant understands the territory is not merely a dot, base, rock, or backdrop for sovereignty theatre.

The contestant may not describe biodiversity as “a nice bonus.”

The biodiversity was there before the opinion column.

The point is simple:

> If you claim to love the territory, you should be able to notice what lives there.

Failure to document local ecosystems respectfully may result in immediate transfer to the remedial module:

The Map Is Not The Habitat, Colin.

## Cocktail Flag Accountability

Every issued cocktail flag must be accounted for.

At the end of the journey, production will ask:

> Where is the flag?

Acceptable answers include:

- “Still in the packet because local consent was not granted.”
- “Returned unused because the claim was more complicated than I had suggested.”
- “Confiscated by customs, which I now understand to be a feature of sovereignty.”
- “Photographed at the agreed location after permission was obtained and nobody was annoyed.”

Unacceptable answers include:

- “I stuck it in something and ran.”
- “A penguin has it.”
- “The locals were being difficult.”
- “The Foreign Office is sorting it.”
- “I dropped it while tweeting.”
- “I believe the flag is spiritually present.”
- “The sea took it, which is legally similar to retention.”

Contestants are reminded that a cocktail flag is not a treaty instrument.

It is cake furniture.

Please behave accordingly.

## No Refunds, No Extraction

There are no refunds.

If contestants are removed from an island, refused entry, stuck at customs, asked to leave by local authorities, ignored by penguins, mugged by a monkey, or told that their cocktail flag is not recognised as a constitutional instrument, that is part of the learning experience.

The Foreign Office is not coming to get you because you attempted global maritime statecraft with:

- a flask of tea;
- a laminated map;
- twelve Jubilee cake flags;
- and the confidence of a man who has never had to arrange port access.

You wanted global reach.

Reach globally.

## Optional Bonus Task — Patriotic Content Capture

Contestants may submit a short video of themselves standing respectfully near the approved location, holding one tiny Union Jack cocktail flag, and singing the national anthem in a manner that does not annoy residents, wildlife, customs officers, port staff, penguins, monkeys, seabirds, turtles, or any nearby person trying to have a normal day.

This is optional.

It is not evidence of sovereignty.

It is not a substitute for treaty literacy.

It does not count as local consent.

It does not resolve fuel, port access, legal authority, defence obligations, displaced populations, maintenance, environmental protection, or the fact that the contestant is still damp.

Production may reject submissions where the contestant:

- sings at protected wildlife;
- films without local permission;
- blocks a road, jetty, path, port entrance, nesting area, memorial, customs queue, or beach access point;
- describes the video as “soft power” without first explaining who asked for it;
- attempts to make a penguin salute;
- uses the cocktail flag as a microphone;
- shouts “for king and country” at a monkey;
- captions the video “mission accomplished” while standing next to a sign saying “do not enter.”

Contestants are reminded:

> A TikTok is not a constitutional instrument.

It is content.

Please stop confusing content with governance.

If your claim to territory depends on singing at it for engagement, your argument may have left the Foreign Affairs track and entered Britain’s Got Geostrategy.

A contestant who declines the itinerary may still have opinions.

Quiet ones.

Preferably indoors.

## Working Principle

The itinerary is not punishment.

It is a stress-test.

Some political claims sound simple only because the speaker has removed:

- distance;
- weather;
- people;
- law;
- ecology;
- local authority;
- customs;
- consent;
- repair;
- fuel;
- port access;
- history;
- displacement;
- and the fact that sovereignty is not activated by cake decorations.

The route puts those things back.

If the claim survives, excellent.

If the claim collapses somewhere between Bermuda and a penguin complaint, the claim was not ready.
