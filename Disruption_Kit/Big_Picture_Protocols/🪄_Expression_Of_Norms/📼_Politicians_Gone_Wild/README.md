# 📼 Politicians Gone Wild  
**SATIRE. SATIRE. SATIRE.**  
*If this programme appears to be a serious policy proposal, please sit quietly, have a biscuit, and wait for an adult with media literacy.*

## Welcome To The Show

**Politicians Gone Wild** is a strictly satirical civic education programme for public figures who have developed strong opinions about suffering they do not personally experience.

Contestants may include:

- politicians;
- columnists;
- think-tank lads;
- retired men with maps;
- lobbyists who say “hard choices” while expensing lunch;
- anyone who says “just common sense” immediately before describing a logistics problem they do not understand.

The format is simple.

A public figure makes a loud opinion.

We remove the velvet cushion from underneath them.

Then we ask:

> Right. Could you personally survive the thing you are recommending for everyone else?

No actual politicians are harmed in the making of this folder.

They may, however, be issued with a dinghy.

---

## Programme Ethos

This is not cruelty.

This is Britain.

We have put children in front of waxwork amputations since 1994 and called it “heritage learning.” We can cope with a joke about a minister being asked to operate a compass.

The house rule is:

> If you can prescribe it, you can explain it.  
> If you can explain it, you can model it.  
> If you cannot model it, stop shouting at people who are already tired.

## Season One: Flag It Yourself

For contestants who believe Britain must personally retain every distant dot on the map because otherwise Nelson’s ghost will get sad.

### Equipment

Each contestant receives:

- one inflatable dinghy;
- one life vest;
- one packet of plain biscuits;
- one 500ml bottle of water;
- one compass;
- one laminated map;
- twelve tiny Union Jack cocktail flags;
- one biro;
- one form titled **“Please Explain What A Supply Chain Is.”**

### Challenge

Contestants must personally travel to every island, base, strategic rock, former imperial node, maritime fantasy object, and suspiciously pink historical map-dot they believe Britain must keep forever.

Luxury travel is banned.

No business class.  
No naval escort.  
No special adviser carrying the snacks.  
No saying “geostrategic” until you have successfully inflated the dinghy.

### Lesson

A flag is not a logistics plan.

A map is not a navy.

A base is not useful because it gives someone in a blazer feelings.

Big boat can get more petrol now.  
Big boat also needs crew, maintenance, port access, legal cover, fuel, repairs, food, water, sanitation, spare parts, allies, and people who know how boats work.

If your maritime strategy collapses when someone asks about petrol, it was not a maritime strategy.

It was a colouring-in exercise with delusions of empire.

## Season Two: Rationing Before Sanctions

For contestants who believe benefit sanctions, hunger, fear, or administrative punishment will improve the moral fibre of poor people.

### Equipment

Each contestant receives:

- one food budget they previously described as “manageable”;
- one online form that crashes at 87%;
- one appointment letter sent to the wrong address;
- one bus route that no longer exists;
- one unexplained deduction;
- one call centre queue;
- one child needing school shoes;
- one fridge containing half an onion and a sense of national decline.

### Challenge

Contestants must live under the conditions they recommend.

No savings.  
No family bailout.  
No assistant.  
No private healthcare.  
No “just get an Uber.”  
No second fridge.  
No partner quietly absorbing the consequences.  
No newspaper column about resilience written from a heated kitchen extension.

### Lesson

Hunger is not a policy instrument.

Poverty is not an obedience course.

If the policy only works because someone else is suffering out of sight, the policy has not been morally tested. It has merely been outsourced.

## Season Three: Govern Like You Mean It

For contestants who believe public services are lazy, inefficient, bloated, indulgent, or “too generous.”

### Episodes May Include

- **Find An NHS Dentist: The Quest For The Holy Filling**
- **Hospital Appointment With Two Children And No Car**
- **Public Transport To A 9am Assessment**
- **Universal Credit Journal Speedrun**
- **Council Phone Line Boss Battle**
- **Housing Form: Final Form**
- **Try Being Ill On A Zero-Hours Contract**
- **School Shoes, Three Days Before Payday**
- **The Printer Is Broken And The Deadline Was Yesterday**
- **The Lift Is Out And You Are Disabled Now**

### Lesson

Administration is not neutral when only some people can afford to escape it.

---

## Season Four: Border Force But Make It Personal

For contestants who talk about migration as if the sea is a queue, a spreadsheet, or a moral failing.

### Equipment

- one cheap coat;
- one plastic bag of documents;
- one phone battery at 9%;
- one language barrier;
- one confused child;
- one border rule changed yesterday;
- one person on television calling you an invasion.

### Challenge

Contestants must explain where, exactly, dignity is supposed to go when a human being becomes a symbol.

### Lesson

Borders are legal realities.

They are not magic lines that make bodies stop needing food, warmth, safety, paperwork, translation, sleep, medicine, rescue, or due process.

## Production Notes

This folder is satire.

It is not a threat.  
It is not a plan.  
It is not an incitement.  
It is not a proposal to drown, starve, abandon, kidnap, endanger, harass, or publicly torment anyone.

It is a joke with a civics degree.

The point is to expose the distance between rhetoric and consequence.

## Core Rule

> No one gets to make pain sound tidy.

Not poverty.  
Not borders.  
Not war.  
Not disability.  
Not hunger.  
Not exile.  
Not boats.  
Not islands.  
Not paperwork.  
Not “efficiency savings.”  
Not “just one more form.”

If your policy requires other people to live inside the consequences, the least you can do is understand the room you are locking them in.
