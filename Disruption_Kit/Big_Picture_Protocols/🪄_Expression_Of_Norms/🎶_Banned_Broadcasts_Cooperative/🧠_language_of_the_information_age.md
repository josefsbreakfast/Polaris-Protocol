It formed inside elite, militarised, and academic environments, where shorthand, metaphor, and omission functioned as cultural glue for insiders. Over time, this linguistic compression hardened into **mystique**: systems that govern daily life but are legible only to a priesthood.

This node documents that dynamic and offers a **counter-reading list** â€” not to romanticise transparency, but to restore civic legibility, security literacy, and democratic agency in the information age.

## ğŸ§© The Mystique Problem (Brief)

* Shorthand replaces explanation ("C", "kernel", "cloud", "AI")
* Metaphor obscures material reality ("immaterial", "virtual", "smart")
* Documentation assumes prior induction
* Ignorance is framed as personal failure, not structural design

The result is a public that is blamed for not understanding systems it was never taught how to see.
