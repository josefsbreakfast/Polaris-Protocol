## 🛰️ Orientation  

This node uses *Does Your Mother Know* by ABBA (1979) as a **cultural anchor** to interrogate contemporary claims that male attraction to significantly younger partners is purely instinctual, inevitable, or historically uncontested.

The track presents a clear, mainstream example of:
- adult awareness of power imbalance  
- expectation of restraint  
- and socially legible boundary-setting  

> The core premise:  
> **Adult responsibility in asymmetrical dynamics was already understood at scale.**

## 🔍 Signal vs Spin  

### Signal (embedded in the song)  
- Attraction is acknowledged  
- Imbalance is recognised  
- The adult declines  

The framing is not tragic or conflicted—it is **matter-of-fact boundary setting**.

---

### Spin (modern reframing)  
- “Men are biologically wired this way”  
- “Youth is peak desirability, therefore pursuit is natural”  
- Responsibility is subtly displaced from the older party  

---

### Counterpoint  

If these behaviours were:
- purely instinctual  
- socially uncontested  
- and universally accepted  

then mainstream media would not:
- model restraint  
- normalise refusal  
- or encode the expectation of adult responsibility  

> The existence of widely accepted counter-examples demonstrates behavioural variability and social expectation.

## 🧠 Terms & Conditions Gap  

A central asymmetry in these dynamics is not desire—but **risk comprehension**.

### Younger party  
- operating on **headline expectations** (romance, excitement, validation)  
- limited exposure to **failure modes** (manipulation, coercion, disrespect, harm)  
- incomplete pattern recognition  

---

### Older party  
- aware of **full risk landscape**  
- capable of anticipating likely outcomes  
- able to influence or steer interactions  

---

### Conclusion  

> **The ethical burden rests with the party who understands the “small print.”**

This is not about restricting teenage emotion or curiosity.  
It is about recognising **asymmetry of knowledge, experience, and consequence**.
