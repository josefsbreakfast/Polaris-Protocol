## 4. Emotive Songs as Coordination Technologies

Songs like *The Internationale* operate differently from speeches or essays:

- they compress values into memory  
- they synchronise emotion and tempo  
- they lower cognitive overhead for collective action  
- they encourage simultaneity rather than deliberation  

This makes them **especially effective**, but also **especially threatening** to large systems that depend on variance reduction and individualised engagement.

This is not unique to leftist songs.  
Many national anthems are equally violent, martial, or absolutist â€” they are simply normalised through state endorsement.

The distinction is not brutality.  
It is **who controls the emotional synchronisation**.

## 6. Why Songs Are More Vulnerable Than Speeches

Speeches:
- are linear  
- require attention  
- are cognitively heavy  
- fragment easily  

Songs:
- propagate horizontally  
- bypass argument  
- synchronise bodies as well as beliefs  

As a result, emotive songs are:
- more likely to be dampened  
- more likely to be mislabelled  
- more likely to be â€œhandledâ€ rather than debated  

This is a structural property of media systems, not a moral judgement.

## 7. Why This Case Matters

This case illustrates a broader pattern:

Understanding this pattern is essential for:
- democratic resilience  
- media literacy  
- distinguishing suppression from disagreement
