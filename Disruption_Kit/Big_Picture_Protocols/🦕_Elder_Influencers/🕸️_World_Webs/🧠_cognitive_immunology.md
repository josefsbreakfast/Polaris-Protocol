### 1. **From Individual Literacy to Population Immunity**

Traditional models treat misinformation resistance as an individual skill:

Cognitive immunology reframes this.

At scale:
- no individual checks everything  
- no one has full context

- recognise implausible claims  
- question automated authority

### 2. **Herd Effects in Epistemic Health**

Cognitive immunity depends on thresholds.

Above the threshold:
- false claims stall  
- errors are corrected  
- manipulation struggles to propagate  

Below the threshold:
- misinformation spreads rapidly  
- authoritative tone replaces accuracy  
- epistemic contagion becomes self-sustaining  

The danger zone is not ignorance â€” it is **partial understanding combined with exhaustion**.

### 3. **Burnout as Immune Suppression**

- sustained attention  
- working memory  
- sceptical pause  
- willingness to intervene  

In burned-out populations:
- people disengage rather than challenge  
- errors are noticed but not acted on  
- â€œsomeone else will checkâ€ becomes default

### 4. **Disinformation, AI Misuse, and Opportunistic Infection**

- they spread through trusted channels  
- they mutate to bypass resistance  

LLMs intensify this dynamic by:
- producing fluent but uncertain outputs  
- collapsing the signal of confidence and correctness  
- accelerating spread beyond human review capacity
