## 🛰️ Orientation  

This node tracks key Russian military and coercive actions across:

- Ukraine  
- Chechnya / North Caucasus  
- the wider Caucasus  

It is not a full history.

It is a **pattern trace**:

> repeated use of force, stabilisation, and consolidation across contested regions.

---

## 👁️ Core Mechanism  

Russian operations follow a recurring pattern:

- initial incursion or destabilisation  
- partial consolidation or territorial control  
- prolonged low-intensity conflict or repression  
- reframing as stabilised or resolved  

> conflict rarely ends—it is reclassified.

---

## 🇺🇦 Ukraine  

- **2014 Mar** — Annexation of Crimea; conflict begins in Donbas.  
- **2015–2021** — Sustained low-intensity conflict; front stabilises.  
- **2022 Feb 24** — Full-scale invasion begins.  
- **2023 May** — Russian forces capture Bakhmut after prolonged fighting.  
- **2024 Feb 17–18** — Ukrainian withdrawal from Avdiivka.  
- **2024 May 10** — Offensive operations toward Kharkiv region.  
- **2024–2025** — Positional warfare; sustained drone and missile campaigns.  

---

## 🪖 Chechnya / North Caucasus  

- **2009–2017** — Counter-insurgency operations degrade organised resistance.  
- **2017–2023** — Consolidation under Kadyrov; internal repression intensifies.  
- **2024 Mar** — Security operations against militants in Ingushetia.  

---

## 🗺️ Wider Caucasus  

- **2011–present** — Continued entrenchment in Abkhazia and South Ossetia; “borderisation.”  
- **2020 Nov** — Russian peacekeepers deployed to Nagorno-Karabakh.  
- **2023 Sep** — Azerbaijan retakes Karabakh; Russian forces remain passive.  
- **2024 Apr 17** — Withdrawal of Russian peacekeepers begins.  

---

## 🔁 Pattern Characteristics  

Across regions, common features include:

- ambiguity between war and stability  
- prolonged “frozen” or semi-frozen conflicts  
- gradual consolidation rather than decisive resolution  
- use of presence to shape political outcomes  

> control is often maintained through persistence rather than decisive victory.
