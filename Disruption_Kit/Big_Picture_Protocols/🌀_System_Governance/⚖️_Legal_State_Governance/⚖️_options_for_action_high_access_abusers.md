## üßø Analysis / Content  

### Tier 0 ‚Äî Evidence Locking (Immediate, Free)
**Purpose:** preserve timelines before behaviour shifts.

- Create a contemporaneous log (dates, platforms, witnesses, symptoms).
- Preserve devices, headers, access notifications, and invoices.
- Platform abuse reports to timestamp patterns (not to ‚Äúwin‚Äù moderation).

> Output: immutable time anchors.

---

### Tier 1 ‚Äî Paper-Forcing (Cheap, High Yield)

#### 1. Subject Access Requests (SARs)
**Targets:** law firms, PR/reputation firms, investigators, data brokers, and relevant public bodies.  
**Ask for:** all personal data; metadata; sources; sharing; profiling; automated decision-making; retention.

**Why it works:** partial disclosures expose **third-party instructions** and **data provenance gaps**.

#### 2. Data Protection Complaints
File where SARs reveal unlawful basis, excessive scope, or ‚Äúsource unknown‚Äù claims.  
**Goal:** compel explanations and audits you cannot otherwise access.

#### 3. Harassment Notice / Cease-and-Desist
Low-cost notice to instigator **and** intermediaries.  
**Effect:** establishes knowledge; tightens later harassment tests.

---

### Tier 2 ‚Äî Behavioural Brakes (Moderate)

#### 4. Police Report (Strategic)
Even without immediate action, you obtain:
- Crime reference number
- Locked timeline
- Escalation leverage for civil claims

#### 5. Regulatory Complaints
Professional regulators (legal, investigative, medical, academic, security) care deeply about:
- Purpose creep
- Client instruction records
- Conflicts of interest

**Effect:** insurer notification; reputational risk; internal reviews.

---

### Tier 3 ‚Äî Disclosure Pressure (Heavy, Decisive)

#### 6. Pre-Action Protocol Letters
Address **instigator + agents** simultaneously.  
Signals readiness for discovery and joint liability arguments.

#### 7. Civil Claims
- **Harassment / stalking:** injunctions + disclosure  
- **Misuse of private information / data protection:** provenance + damages

**Key doctrine:** procuring or aiding misconduct defeats ‚ÄúI didn‚Äôt do it personally‚Äù.

---

### Tier 4 ‚Äî Public-Interest Escalation (Situational)

#### 8. Judicial Review
Where public systems or privileged access were misused for private ends.  
**Outcome:** policy exposure; access log scrutiny; institutional remedies.

#### 9. Whistleblowing Channels
Protected disclosures can surface instructions, logs, or invoices even when the target lacks standing.
