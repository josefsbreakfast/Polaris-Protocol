## ü™º What Schmittian Drift Looks Like  
Schmitt‚Äôs sovereign claims legitimacy by determining when the law is suspended.  
In liberal democracy, the opposite contract exists: legality restrains the sovereign.

Schmittian drift occurs when:

- emergency powers persist beyond emergency conditions,  
- classification replaces evidence,  
- suspicion replaces charge,  
- security rationales replace judicial scrutiny,  
- pretrial exceptionalism becomes indistinguishable from punishment.

This drift is structural, not necessarily intentional.

When prolonged, it transforms the meaning of innocence itself.
