## üß† Legitimacy Is the First Casualty

Democratic authority depends on a basic bargain:

- the law applies equally  
- power is accountable  
- coercion is justified by fairness  

Elite impunity fractures that bargain.

When citizens observe that:
- powerful actors evade consequence  
- enforcement is asymmetric  
- accountability is performative  

they stop experiencing the state as *legitimate* ‚Äî even if formal democratic structures remain intact.

## ‚öñÔ∏è The Justice Vacuum

Selective non-prosecution creates a vacuum where:

- wrongdoing is visible  
- punishment is absent  
- explanations are unconvincing  

Vacuum does not remain empty.

It invites actors willing to offer:
- certainty instead of ambiguity  
- force instead of process  
- punishment instead of justice  

Authoritarian movements do not invent this space.  
They **occupy it**.

## üîÅ The Predictable Escalation Pattern

The pattern is consistent across contexts:

1. elite wrongdoing is exposed  
2. institutions decline to act decisively  
3. trust in lawful process erodes  
4. anger seeks an outlet  
5. demagogues offer simple blame and harsh solutions  

At this point, the rule of law is already weakened.
Authoritarianism arrives late ‚Äî not early.

## üß© Why ‚ÄúLaw and Order‚Äù Rhetoric Works After Impunity

Authoritarian appeals succeed not because populations suddenly love repression, but because:

- lawful justice appears unavailable  
- proportional accountability seems impossible  
- enforcement looks selective  

In that environment:
> harsh punishment feels fairer than no punishment.

This is how impunity flips into punitive excess.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Impunity Shifts Fear, Not Harm

When elites are protected:
- fear moves downward  
- enforcement targets the visible  
- minorities and outsiders absorb pressure  

This restores a *feeling* of control while leaving the original harm untouched.

Authoritarianism thrives on **misdirected enforcement**.

---

## üèõÔ∏è Institutions Become Tools, Not Constraints

As trust collapses:
- courts are seen as obstacles  
- due process is reframed as weakness  
- safeguards are portrayed as indulgence  

At that point, institutions that once limited power are repurposed to concentrate it.

This transition does not require a coup.
It requires **enough disappointment**.

---

## üß® Why Inquiries Accelerate the Risk

When inquiries repeatedly substitute for prosecution:

- truth is acknowledged without consequence  
- elites survive exposure  
- the public learns restraint applies upward  

This teaches that:
> justice through process is illusory.

Authoritarian narratives then position themselves as the *only* path to accountability.

## üî• The Security Dimension

States weakened by impunity are easier to pressure, manipulate, and divide.

## üßÆ The Bottom Line

Authoritarianism is not born from too much law.

When democratic systems fail to confront elite wrongdoing, they do not preserve moderation.
