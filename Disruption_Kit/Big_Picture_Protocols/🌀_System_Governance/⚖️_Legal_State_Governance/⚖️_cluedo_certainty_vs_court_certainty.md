## âš–ï¸ Court Certainty  

Court certainty is not about truth.  
It is about **survivability inside a hostile procedural environment**.

It requires:
- admissible evidence,
- documented chains of custody,
- witnesses who survive adversarial attack,
- and compliance with rules that are often orthogonal to harm.

Court certainty answers a different question:
> *Can this conclusion withstand motivated challenge inside institutional rules?*

It is not a higher certainty.  
It is a **narrower**, more brittle one.
