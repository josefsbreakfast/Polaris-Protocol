## ‚öñÔ∏è Legal Framework (High Level)

Under the Genocide Convention, states have an obligation to:

- **Prevent genocide**, not merely avoid committing it.
- Act when there is a **serious risk**, even if genocide has not yet been legally determined.
- Use **means reasonably available** to them, proportionate to their influence.

This duty is:
- **Positive** (requires action),
- **Contextual** (depends on capacity and influence),
- **Forward-looking** (not excused by uncertainty).

Failure to prevent can arise through:
- omission,
- delay,
- continuation of material support,
- or refusal to use available leverage.
