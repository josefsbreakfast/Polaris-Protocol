## ðŸ§¿ Analysis  
Representation used to be **relational**.  
A councillor or MP was porous to the public because public life itself created unavoidable encounters.  
When the social fabric thins, the representative becomes a figure who hears only through abstraction.

Listening becomes:  
- sampling, not dialogue  
- metrics, not testimony  
- trends, not relationships  
- noise-filtered dashboards, not living voices  

This creates a democratic illusion: *the appearance* of responsiveness without the presence of the public.

In this architecture, discontent becomes invisible until it catastrophically breaches containment.  
The quieter the constituency becomes, the more brittle the governance that oversees it.

Re-hearing the politic will require rebuilding **embodied, non-transactional, analogue spaces** where people and representatives share time, risk, and context.
