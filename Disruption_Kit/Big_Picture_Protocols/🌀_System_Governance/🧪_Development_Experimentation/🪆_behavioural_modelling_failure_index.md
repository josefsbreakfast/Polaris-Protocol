This index node provides a **structured overview** of the behavioural modelling failure modes documented in this sequence of Polaris nodes.

It exists to:

- situate individual failures in relation to one another

The failures described here are **systemic**, not anomalous.

---

## The core pattern

Across modern behavioural modelling systems, the same pattern recurs:

1. **Shallow proxies** are used to infer complex human behaviour  
2. **Sparse or skewed data** is over-interpreted  
3. **Legacy representations** persist beyond their validity  
4. **Research artefacts** escape containment  
5. **Consent boundaries** are silently crossed  
6. **Sensitive inference** is normalised  
7. **Relational narratives** fill conceptual gaps  
8. **Explanation substitutes for accountability**  

Each step is individually explainable.  
Together, they produce **structural harm**.

## What this index is not

This index does **not** argue that:

- systems are malicious  
- individuals are targeted  
- failures are conspiratorial  
- intent explains harm  

The failures documented here arise from:
- convenience  
- abstraction  
- legacy practice  
- organisational inertia  
- weak governance  

They are dangerous precisely because they are ordinary.

---

## Why this index exists

Without a map, these failures are often treated as:

- unrelated bugs  
- edge cases  
- unfortunate coincidences  
- user misunderstanding  
- acceptable trade-offs  

This index exists to make clear that they are **connected**, **predictable**, and **preventable**.

Understanding the structure is a prerequisite for accountability.
