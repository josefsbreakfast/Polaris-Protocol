# 🕊️ Positive Drift  
**First created:** 2025-11-08 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-08  
*Small rotations that change the tone of an entire field.*  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  
Late 2025 saw a quiet but significant refresh of senior posts in UK counter-terrorism and in agencies previously influenced by U.S.-style Heritage-aligned networks.  
The rotation didn’t come with slogans or manifestos; its importance lies in what it replaced — a generation of *status-quo stewards* who treated risk and culture as exercises in reputation management.

Polaris marks this as a **positive drift**: evidence that the civic immune system still works, however slowly.

---

## 🌀 From Heritage Patronage to Civic Competence  

| Old Guard Logic | Emerging Logic |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Appointments via trans-Atlantic ideological networks | Appointments by demonstrable procedural skill |
| Security as spectacle or moral theatre | Security as balanced prevention and rights protection |
| Heritage-style donor politics | Public-interest governance |
| Narratives of control | Narratives of transparency |

These aren’t new doctrines; they’re simply signs that the people making the calls now *read the room* differently.

## 🌱 Why It Matters  
Every generational shuffle chips away at inherited opacity.  
A few competent appointments at the top can cascade into safer policy, less grandstanding, and a public sphere that finally rewards *doing the work* instead of *guarding the brand*.

The positive drift is not revolution; it’s the slow rotation that stops systems from fossilising.

---

## 🦤 Context Note — The Heritage Foundation  

The **Heritage Foundation** is a U.S. conservative policy think-tank founded in 1973, long aligned with Republican administrations.  
Its model of governance emphasises ideological loyalty, donor influence, and media choreography over public-sector procedure.  
Several UK security and policy figures maintained informal links or shared networks with Heritage-aligned initiatives during the 2010s–2020s.  
The current reshuffle moves authority away from those circles toward **civic-professional** leadership — a shift from ideological performance to administrative competence.
