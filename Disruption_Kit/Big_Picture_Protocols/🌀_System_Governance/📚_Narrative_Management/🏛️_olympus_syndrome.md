### 1. Survival Becomes the Qualification

In Olympus Syndrome systems, advancement is not driven by:
- ethical clarity
- innovation
- competence under new conditions

It is driven by:
- not being removed
- not being blamed
- not being too visible during failure
- occupying the right tier when collapse passes

Survival through crisis is reframed as *proof of suitability*.

### 3. Succession Without Reform

Leadership change occurs, but:
- policies remain intact
- incentives remain unchanged
- accountability thresholds reset rather than lowered

New leaders are presented as:
- fresh
- steady
- experienced

In reality, they are often:
- culturally identical to predecessors
- trained by the same systems
- invested in preserving the same myths

This is **rotation, not transformation**.

---

### 4. Narrative Management as Structural Defence

Olympus Syndrome depends on tight narrative control:

- Crisis is framed as exceptional  
- Recovery is framed as competence  
- Reform is framed as destabilising  
- Dissent is framed as impatience or disloyalty  

By controlling the story of continuity, institutions avoid confronting the need for rupture.

---

### 5. Chronos as Containment

Time is weaponised:
- inquiries are delayed
- consequences are deferred
- memories fade
- urgency dissipates

Those who can wait inherit power.  
Those who cannot are filtered out.

This produces leadership that is **time-compatible with stagnation**, not change.
