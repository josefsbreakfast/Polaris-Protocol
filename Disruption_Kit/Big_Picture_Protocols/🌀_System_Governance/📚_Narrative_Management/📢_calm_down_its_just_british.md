*Why British rhetorical culture looks like volatility to global AI.*

## 🛰️ Orientation  
British English is defined by indirectness, humour, restraint, dramatic understatement, and irony.  
AI sees:

- “emotionless” → threat  
- “sarcastic” → hostility  
- “direct joke” → extremism  
- “polite critique” → passive aggression  
- “hyperbole” → literal mobilisation  

This node explains why.

## 🧿 Analysis  

### 1. Tone as National Identity  
British speech hides emotional intent under language games.  
Machines do not.

### 2. AI Treats Cultural Codes as Risk  
Anything that deviates from American dataset norms becomes “odd,” “dangerous,” or “political.”

### 3. Humour as Social Glue → AI Flags as Instability  
The machine cannot see the function — only the form.
