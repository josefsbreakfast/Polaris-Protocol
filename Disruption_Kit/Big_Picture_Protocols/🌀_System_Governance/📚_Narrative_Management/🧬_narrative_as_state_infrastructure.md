## ğŸ§¿ Analysis

### 1. Narrative Precedes Policy

Before a policy can exist, a story must exist:

* who is dangerous
* who is vulnerable
* who must be protected
* what counts as a threat

These stories determine the *policy surface area* â€” the range of actions that feel reasonable.

---

### 2. Risk Framing as Infrastructure

Risk is not a neutral descriptor. It is a narrative act.

Risk framing:

* selects certain futures as plausible
* weights certain harms over others
* privileges pre-emption over repair

Once embedded, risk narratives justify extraordinary intervention while appearing technocratic.

---

### 3. Fear Framing and Urgency

Fear compresses time.

Fear-based narratives:

* shorten deliberation windows
* elevate security over rights
* reward visible action
* penalise uncertainty or restraint

Fear thus becomes a **policy accelerator**.

---

### 4. Identity Framing

Narratives assign meaning to identity:

* some identities are framed as risk-bearing
* others as neutral or default
* some as needing management

When identity becomes a risk signal, governance shifts from behaviour to *being*.

---

### 5. The Inward Turn

Narrative tools developed for:

* foreign policy
* war mobilisation
* counter-insurgency

become destabilising when applied domestically.

Citizens are reframed as populations, dissent as threat, and care as containment.

---

### 6. Persistence and Stickiness

Narratives outlast evidence.

Even when facts change:

* frames persist
* institutions align around them
* reversal becomes politically costly

Narrative infrastructure hardens into governance reality.
