# 🎴 Tarot of Prime Ministers  
**First created:** 2025-11-16 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-13  
*A symbolic tarot-style mapping of recent UK Prime Ministers as archetypes — not of their politics, but of their narrative energy, institutional impact, and governance “vibe”.*  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  
This is **not** political endorsement, condemnation, or literal analysis.  
It is an *archetypal mapping exercise* that treats Prime Ministers as symbolic figures within the institutional mood of their era — using tarot’s narrative language as the framing device.

The tarot references here are literary and metaphorical, not spiritual claims.

Each PM is assigned a card that reflects how their governance *felt culturally and narratively*, not how it objectively performed.

---

## 🎴 Major Arcana Assignments

### **Tony Blair — The Magician (I)**  
**Keywords:** charisma, illusion, narrative mastery, transformation  

Blair embodied the archetype of political alchemy — new image, new language, new story.  

Light side: modernisation.  
Shadow side: illusion, overreach, the spell breaking.

---

### **Gordon Brown — The Hermit (IX)**  
**Keywords:** seriousness, introspection, duty, isolation  

A cerebral, often isolated figure carrying the lamp of economic caution.  
Served as a tonal counterweight to Blair’s magician energy.

---

### **David Cameron — The Fool (0)**  
**Keywords:** naïveté, leaps without foresight, unintended consequences  

The Fool steps toward the cliff believing the world will catch him.  
The referendum era gave this archetype unusual symbolic resonance.

---

### **Theresa May — Justice (XI)**  
**Keywords:** order, procedure, impossible balances  

Her era became associated with proceduralism, institutional strain, and attempts to restore balance using increasingly fragile tools.

---

### **Boris Johnson — The Tower (XVI)**  
**Keywords:** chaos, collapse, revelation, hubris  

The Tower represents systems destabilised by accumulated contradictions.  
Johnson’s premiership strongly evoked that imagery in public culture.

---

### **Liz Truss — The Eight of Wands (Minor Arcana)**  
**Keywords:** speed, recklessness, sudden movement, volatility  

Not Major Arcana — the period was too brief.  
Pure acceleration energy with little stabilisation.

---

### **Rishi Sunak — The Hanged Man (XII)**  
**Keywords:** suspended stasis, sacrifice, immobility  

A PM often perceived as governing within conditions he could not meaningfully reshape.  
Suspended governance became the dominant symbolic mood.

---

### **Keir Starmer — The Hierophant (V)**  
**Keywords:** order, procedure, institutional restoration  

Not romantic.  
Not theatrical.  
An archetype of administrative normalisation and procedural restoration.

Represents the return of rules, compliance, and predictable governance.
