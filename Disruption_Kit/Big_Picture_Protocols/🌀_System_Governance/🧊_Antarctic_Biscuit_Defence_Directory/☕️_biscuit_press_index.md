# 🛰️ Orientation  

The **Biscuit Press** series functions as the recurring media layer of the Antarctic Biscuit Defence Directorate mythos.  

Structured as a rolling archive of:
- press releases,
- damage-control statements,
- headline roundups,
- public relations catastrophes,
- leaked editorial chaos,
- and increasingly implausible explanations,

the series satirises:
- institutional communications,
- bureaucratic self-preservation,
- military-adjacent public relations,
- and the tendency of organisations to aestheticise dysfunction rather than resolve it.

The humour operates through:
- escalation,
- deadpan administrative tone,
- contradiction between official statements and observable reality,
- and the collision of serious governmental language with deeply unserious operational crises.

While comedic in form, the structure mirrors recognisable patterns found in:
- crisis communications,
- media spin cycles,
- reputation management,
- bureaucratic opacity,
- and institutional narrative containment.

# ✨ Key Features  

## 🧾 Institutional Satire  

The series parodies:
- official communications structures,
- crisis-management rhetoric,
- and the language of “professional reassurance” under obvious systemic failure.

---

## 🐧 Escalating Operational Absurdity  

Each release expands the ABDD setting through:
- contradictory explanations,
- increasingly unstable logistics,
- rogue operational actors,
- and media ecosystems attempting to maintain composure.

---

## 📺 Narrative Management Parody  

The press format allows satire of:
- press briefings,
- reputation laundering,
- strategic ambiguity,
- selective disclosure,
- and public-facing containment language.

---

## ☕️ Administrative Deadpan  

Much of the humour relies on:
- calm procedural tone,
- bureaucratic formatting,
- and the refusal of official language to emotionally acknowledge catastrophic absurdity.
