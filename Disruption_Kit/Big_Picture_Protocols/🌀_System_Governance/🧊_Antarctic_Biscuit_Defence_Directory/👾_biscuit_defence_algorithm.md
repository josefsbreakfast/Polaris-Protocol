## 🧮 Core Architecture  

The ABDD predictive engine (“**CookieNet v0.1**”) is designed to provide:
> *real-time snack threat classification.*  

---

### 📥 Inputs  

CookieNet continuously ingests telemetry from:

- mobile phones,  
- smart kettles,  
- toasters,  
- supermarket loyalty cards,  
- vending machines,  
- and CCTV-derived dunk-velocity analysis systems.  

Additional behavioural markers include:
- biscuit-purchase frequency,
- tea-to-biscuit ratio,
- and unauthorised cracker proximity events.  

---

### ⚙️ Model Structure  

CookieNet operates using a:
- linear binary classifier.  

Outputs are restricted to:

- **0 = Loyal**  
- **1 = Stealer**  

Intermediate values are formally categorised as:
> “operational weakness.”  

Attempts to introduce nuance into the model architecture were rejected for:
- “excessive softness.”  

---

### 📤 Outputs  

#### 🟢 No Stealer Detected  

- Subject logged and ignored until next tea cycle.  

- Behaviour archived for future suspicion.  

---

#### 🔴 Stealer Detected  

Subject may be:
- tagged,
- redirected,
- nudged,
- monitored,
- or egged,
depending on threat level and available biscuits.  

---

## ⚠️ Known Limitations  

### 📈 Bias Amplification  

CookieNet assumes:
> biscuits are the universal cultural good.  

Subjects consuming:
- naan,
- baguettes,
- tortillas,
- or non-standard carbohydrate structures
are flagged as:
> “foreign stealer risk.”  

---

### ☕️ Gendered Dunking Error  

Strong dunking behaviour is automatically classified as:
- masculine-coded,
- aggressive,
- and strategically suspicious.  

Escalation thresholds remain poorly calibrated.  

---

### 💥 Intersectional Catastrophe  

Mixed-signal inputs such as:

- woman  
+ tortilla  
+ strong dunk  

trigger:
> **CRITICAL ALERT**  

Typical system response includes:
- dashboard freezing,
- kettle alarms,
- and emergency biscuit deployment recommendations.

## 📡 Interference Toolkit  

CookieNet integrates multiple surveillance-adjacent behavioural systems.  

---

### 🗺️ Content Injection  

Google Maps routes replaced with:
- snack aisle detours,
- morale corridors,
- and “accidental” supermarket loops.  

---

### 📲 Algorithmic Nudging  

Suspected biscuit-stealers receive:
- targeted Hobnob advertisements,
- motivational snack messaging,
- and emotionally manipulative tea content
until behavioural compliance improves.  

---

### 🧾 Metadata Garnishing  

Fake crumbs are strategically inserted into:
- data trails,
- purchase histories,
- and snack analytics
to entrap suspected actors.  

---

### 📺 Digital Dunk Jamming  

A two-second lag is introduced into:
- YouTube tea tutorials,
- dunking videos,
- and livestream biscuit reviews
to destabilise hostile snack coordination.
