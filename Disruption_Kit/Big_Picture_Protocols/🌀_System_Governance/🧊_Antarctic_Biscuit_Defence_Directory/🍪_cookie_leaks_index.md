## 🛰️ Orientation  

The **CookieLeaks** series functions as the unofficial disclosure archive of the Antarctic Biscuit Defence Directorate (ABDD).  

Structured as a rolling collection of:
- leaks,
- internal reports,
- operational mishaps,
- social media failures,
- cover-ups,
- and procedural catastrophes,

the series satirises:
- institutional secrecy,
- bureaucratic self-preservation,
- crisis management culture,
- and the tendency of organisations to reinterpret embarrassment as strategy.

The archive format mirrors:
- whistleblower ecosystems,
- intelligence leaks,
- internal audit disclosures,
- and fragmented institutional memory systems,
where:
> the unofficial record becomes more revealing than the official one.

Across the series, ABDD attempts to maintain:
- operational authority,
- procedural dignity,
- and narrative control,
while simultaneously being undermined by:
- hashtags,
- vending machines,
- wildlife,
- livestreams,
- interns,
- and deeply avoidable decisions.

## ✨ Key Features  

### 📁 Leak Archive Structure  

The CookieLeaks format parodies:
- classified disclosures,
- internal investigations,
- and institutional archive systems
that inadvertently preserve the very failures they attempt to contain.

---

### 🧾 Bureaucratic Reframing  

Operational disasters are repeatedly reclassified as:
- resilience exercises,
- perimeter enforcement,
- outreach activities,
- or morale operations.

Administrative language becomes a survival mechanism.

---

### 📡 Narrative Control Failure  

The series explores how:
- institutions lose control of public narrative,
particularly once:
- leaks,
- memes,
- screenshots,
- and online culture
begin circulating faster than official responses.

---

### ☕️ Institutional Absurdism  

The ABDD universe depends on organisations continuing to operate:
- procedurally,
- formally,
- and with total administrative seriousness,
even while reality has clearly abandoned cooperation.
