## 🛰️ Orientation  

*🍪 CookieLeaks 004* examines the ABDD’s growing inability to separate:
- operational secrecy,
- internal morale culture,
- and public social media performance.

Structured as a leak archive focused on Instagram-era operational collapse, the node satirises:
- performative professionalism,
- institutional oversharing,
- clout-driven posting culture,
- and the erosion of security boundaries under algorithmic attention systems.

The humour emerges from the contradiction between:
- organisations demanding secrecy,
and:
- personnel instinctively documenting everything for engagement, aesthetics, and social validation.

Within the ABDD setting, operational security fails not because of:
- espionage,
- hacking,
- or infiltration,
but because agents voluntarily curate classified biscuit imagery online.

The node also lightly mirrors real-world:
- OSINT vulnerabilities,
- metadata exposure,
- and security failures created through routine social media behaviour.

## 📂 Incident Archive: Vol. 4 — Social Media Mishaps  

### 📸 CL-015: The Instagram Biscuit Parade  

It was discovered that multiple ABDD agents (codename: ADCC) were regularly posting photographs of operational biscuits to Instagram.  

Images included:
- sensitive biscuit stacks positioned on briefing tables,
- partially visible deployment maps,
- reflective kettle surfaces,
- and identifiable bunker décor visible in the background.  

Hashtags including:

- `#CustardCreamOps`
- `#SnackSecured`
- `#EliteDunkUnit`

rapidly gained traction online.  

Several suspected biscuit-stealers successfully identified:
- biscuit cache locations,
- supply corridors,
- and at least one unsecured storage entrance
through:
- background analysis,
- image reflections,
- and repeated geotagging patterns.  

---

### 🧾 Action Taken  

ABDD leadership instructed personnel to:
- cease operational posting immediately,
- remove publicly visible biscuit deployments,
- and stop referring to snack storage as “the vault.”  

An internal memo later described the issue as:  

> “visibility without deniability.”  

Subsequent review concluded that:
- leadership only recognised the scale of the exposure
after:
- widespread complaints,
- meme circulation,
- and a thirty-seven-post online thread analysing biscuit logistics in forensic detail.
