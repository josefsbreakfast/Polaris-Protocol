**First created:** 2025-09-30 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-14  
*Official ABDD multilingual readiness exercise for agents operating across high-risk international biscuit environments and diplomatically unstable teatime zones.*  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

*🎙️ Biscuit Pronunciation Drills* satirises:
- institutional language training,
- military repetition exercises,
- bureaucratic internationalism,
- and the absurd seriousness with which organisations often approach symbolic preparedness.

Presented as an official ABDD pronunciation exercise, the node combines:
- multilingual biscuit terminology,
- phonetic drills,
- pseudo-military discipline,
- and escalating snack-security paranoia.

The humour emerges from the collision between:
- formal training culture,
and:
- the profoundly unserious subject matter of chanting biscuit names in formation.

At the same time, the piece lightly reflects:
- genuine pronunciation training structures,
- international operational phrasebooks,
- and the complexity of cross-cultural terminology,
especially where:
- false friends,
- loanwords,
- and regional food categories
create opportunities for misunderstanding.

Within the ABDD universe, accurate biscuit pronunciation is treated as:
> a matter of operational readiness and diplomatic stability.
