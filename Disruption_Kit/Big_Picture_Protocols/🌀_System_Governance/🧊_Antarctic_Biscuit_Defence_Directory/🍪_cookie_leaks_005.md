## 📂 Incident Archive: Vol. 5 — Social Media & Field Mishaps  

### 📸 CL-016: The Instagram Biscuit Parade  

It was discovered that multiple ABDD agents (codename: ADCC) were routinely posting photographs of operational biscuits to Instagram.  

Images included:
- sensitive biscuit arrangements,
- briefing-table snack stacks,
- partially visible operational documents,
- and recognisable bunker interiors reflected in kettle surfaces.  

Hashtags including:

- `#CustardCreamOps`
- `#SnackSecured`
- `#TacticalDigestive`

rapidly gained traction online.  

Several suspected biscuit-stealers successfully identified:
- biscuit cache locations,
- supply corridors,
- and one unsecured storage annex
through:
- background analysis,
- metadata patterns,
- and geotagged tea photographs.  

#### 🧾 Action Taken  

- Agents were instructed to cease operational posting immediately.  

- Internal guidance prohibited:
  - “aesthetic biscuit deployment photography,”
  - and “classified snack flat-lays.”  

- Leadership later described the incident as:
> “visibility without deniability.”  

Subsequent review determined that:
- senior leadership only understood the scale of the breach
after:
- widespread public complaints,
- meme circulation,
- and repeated reposting by amateur OSINT accounts.  

---

### 🐜 CL-017: The Fire Ant Excursion  

During a “community engagement exercise,” an ABDD squad staged a casual biscuit picnic near a public park.  

Unfortunately:
- the selected location was directly atop an active fire ant colony.  

Within minutes:
- multiple operatives suffered severe allergic reactions,
- field discipline collapsed,
- and several agents abandoned tactical posture entirely.  

Eyewitness reports stated that:
- local children laughed openly
while operatives fled the scene clutching biscuit tins and improvised ice packs.  

#### 🧾 Action Taken  

- Entire unit transported to hospital for precautionary treatment.  

- Official report classified the incident as:
> “natural forces hostile to biscuit sovereignty.”  

- Internal morale survey later ranked the excursion:
> “least soldierly moment of the fiscal year.”  

Recommendations included:
- improved terrain assessment,
- reduced picnic-based operational planning,
- and mandatory ant-awareness briefings.
