# 📖 Biscuit Field Phrasebook  
**First created:** 2025-09-30 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-14  
*A multilingual quick-reference guide for ABDD field operatives attempting to acquire biscuits abroad without triggering diplomatic incidents, sponge confusion, or accidental gravy deployment.*  

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

*📖 Biscuit Field Phrasebook* satirises:
- operational phrasebooks,
- international field manuals,
- bureaucratic travel guidance,
- and the surprisingly fragile geopolitics of food terminology.

Presented as a laminated field reference for ABDD operatives, the node combines:
- multilingual snack vocabulary,
- pronunciation guidance,
- cultural warnings,
- and escalating biscuit-related paranoia.

The humour emerges from treating:
- routine bakery communication
as though it were:
- a delicate international security exercise.

At the same time, the node lightly reflects genuine:
- translation ambiguity,
- false-friend terminology,
- and regional food-classification differences
that can create confusion in multilingual environments.

Within the ABDD universe, the ability to correctly request biscuits abroad is treated as:
> a foundational diplomatic survival skill.

## 📑 Phrases By Language  

| Language | Phrase | Pronunciation | Translation / Notes |
|----------|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| 🇬🇧 English (UK) | “Excuse me, where are the biscuits?” | /ˈbɪs.kɪts/ | Standard query. Expect Digestives, Hobnobs, or Custard Creams. |
| 🇺🇸 English (US) | “Excuse me, where are the cookies?” | /ˈkʊk.iz/ | Use *cookie*, not *biscuit*, unless requesting gravy bread rolls. |
| 🇫🇷 French | “Où sont les biscuits, s’il vous plaît ?” | /u sɔ̃ le bis.kɥi/ | May produce cookies *or* sponge fingers. Specify *biscuits secs* for crisp biscuits. |
| 🇩🇪 German | “Wo sind die Kekse, bitte?” | /voː zɪnt diː ˈkɛk.sə ˈbɪ.tə/ | Clear cookie request. Avoid *Biskuit* unless sponge escalation is desired. |
| 🇪🇸 Spanish | “¿Dónde están las galletas?” | /ˈdon.de esˈtan las ɡaˈʎe.tas/ (Spain), /ɡaˈje.tas/ (LatAm) | Covers cookies and crackers. Clarify *dulces* vs *saladas*. |
| 🇮🇹 Italian | “Dove sono i biscotti?” | /ˈdɔ.ve ˈso.no i bisˈkɔt.ti/ | Safe general request. May include cantucci and other hard biscuits. |
| 🇬🇷 Greek | “Πού είναι τα μπισκότα;” (*Poú eínai ta biskóta?*) | /pu ˈi.ne ta bisˈko.ta/ | General biscuit request. *Κουλουράκια* may appear as bonus snacks. |
| 🇹🇷 Turkish | “Bisküviler nerede?” | /bisˈcy.vi.ler ˈne.re.de/ | Effective for most shops and kiosks. |
| 🇸🇦 Arabic | “أين البسكويت؟” (*Ayna al-baskwīt?*) | /ˈʔaj.na‿l bisˈkwiːt/ | Borrowed modern term. May include crackers or packaged snacks. |
| 🇮🇱 Hebrew | “איפה העוגיות?” (*Eyfo ha-ugiyot?*) | /ˈe.fo ha uɡiˈjot/ | Everyday cookie term. Saying *biskvit* may summon sponge cake. |
| 🕎 Yiddish | “Vu zaynen di kikhl?” | /vu ˈzaj.nən di ˈkɪxl/ | Standard biscuit request. Saying *biskvit* may result in sponge disappointment. |

## ⚠️ Agent Advisory  

### 🇬🇧 vs 🇺🇸 Biscuit Confusion  

Never confuse:
- UK biscuits
with:
- US biscuits.  

One accompanies tea.  
The other arrives covered in gravy.  

---

### 🇪🇸 Spanish Clarification Requirement  

*Galleta salada* refers to savoury crackers.  

Do not:
- present at dessert tables,
- deploy during diplomatic receptions,
- or classify as morale biscuits.  

---

### 🇫🇷 French Sponge Event  

French *biscuit* terminology may produce:
- sponge fingers,
- sponge cake,
- or elegant confusion.  

This is not sabotage.  
This is simply France.  

---

### 🕎 Yiddish Sponge Escalation  

Saying:
> *biskvit*  

in a Yiddish-speaking environment may result in:
- sponge cake,
- emotional confusion,
- and operational disappointment.  

Remain calm.
