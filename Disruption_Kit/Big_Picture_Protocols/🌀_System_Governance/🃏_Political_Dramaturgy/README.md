## 🛰️ Orientation  

**Political Dramaturgy** examines governance as performance.

This cluster studies:
- political aesthetics,
- archetypes,
- symbolic authority,
- charisma,
- public ritual,
- emotional staging,
- and the theatrical dimensions of legitimacy.

It focuses on the ways institutions and leaders become narratively legible through:
- vibe,
- costume,
- symbolism,
- emotional tonality,
- and performative coherence.

This includes both democratic and authoritarian systems.

The question is not merely:
> “Who has power?”

But:
> “What kind of story does power believe it is inside?”

## ⚖️ Governance Notes  

This cluster analyses symbolic systems rather than endorsing them.

Archetypes are used diagnostically:
not as personality destiny, racial theory, or metaphysical truth claims.

Political aesthetics should also not be mistaken for superficiality.

Aesthetic coherence can materially shape:
- legitimacy,
- mobilisation,
- institutional stability,
- emotional trust,
- and public perception of authority.
