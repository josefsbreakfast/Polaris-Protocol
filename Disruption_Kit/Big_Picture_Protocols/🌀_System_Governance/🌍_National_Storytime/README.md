# 🌍 National Storytime  
**First created:** 2026-05-11 | **Last updated:** 2026-05-18  
*How nations narrate themselves into coherence through myth, memory, trauma, and belonging.*

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  

**National Storytime** examines the stories nations tell about themselves.

This cluster studies:
- civilisational mythmaking,
- historical memory,
- borders,
- belonging,
- trauma narratives,
- national self-image,
- and the symbolic fictions required to maintain collective identity.

The focus is not whether these stories are “true” in absolute terms.

The focus is:
> how national narratives organise emotional and political reality.

This includes:
- heroic myths,
- victimhood narratives,
- liberation stories,
- frontier myths,
- democratic mythology,
- and inherited cultural memory.

The cluster approaches nations not as static entities, but as:
- ongoing narrative projects,
- emotionally maintained over time.

## ⚖️ Governance Notes  

This cluster does not treat national narratives as inherently false.

All societies require:
- continuity stories,
- legitimacy myths,
- symbolic histories,
- and emotional frameworks for collective belonging.

The purpose of analysis is not cynicism.

It is:
- interpretive literacy,
- myth awareness,
- and recognition of how identity narratives shape governance behaviour.

Particular care should be taken around:
- ethnic identity,
- religion,
- conflict narratives,
- and historical trauma.
