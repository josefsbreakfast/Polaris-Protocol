# 🦕 Post-Modern Fellow Travellers  
**First created:** 2025-11-16 | **Last updated:** 2026-03-17  
*How non-malicious actors enable authoritarian outcomes inside behavioural-surveillance systems.*

---

## 🛰️ Orientation  
Authoritarian harm in contemporary institutions rarely comes from “true believers.”  
It comes from **ordinary people** — colleagues, managers, safeguarding staff, HR officers, academics, administrators — who never intend harm but whose actions **enable** it.

These are the **fellow travellers** of modern risk architecture:

- people who comply with procedure,  
- people who act “just in case,”  
- people who fear missing a sign,  
- people who trust the system implicitly,  
- people who escalate without understanding the consequences,  
- people who believe vigilance = virtue.

This node explains how harmless intentions become **structural violence** inside Prevent-heavy behavioural-surveillance ecosystems.

## ✨ Key Features  
- Explains the fellow-traveller dynamic in institutional settings.  
- Shows how authoritarian outcomes are produced procedurally, not ideologically.  
- Maps the cognitive mechanisms that drive unnecessary escalation.

- Describes how survivors experience distributed hostility from “neutral” actors.

---

## 🧿 Who Are Fellow Travellers?  
They are not predators, bigots, or extremists.  
They are:

- earnest safeguarding staff,  
- risk-averse managers,  
- overworked admin teams,  
- junior colleagues seeking approval,  
- senior colleagues protecting reputation,  
- staff who “follow procedure,”  
- people who assume the system knows best.

They participate in harm unintentionally.

---

## 👀 Why Fellow Travellers Matter More Than Villains  
Authoritarian systems scale not through conviction but through **compliance**.

Most escalation in Prevent-heavy systems originates from:

- caution,  
- anxiety,  
- ambiguity,  
- fear of blame,  
- misinterpretation,  
- inconsistent communication,  
- following policy without judgement.

Harm doesn’t need an agent — it needs a process.

---

## 👻 Mechanisms That Produce Fellow-Traveller Harm

### **a) Diffusion of Responsibility**  
“I’m not accusing anyone — I’m just passing this on.”

### **b) Ambiguity Inflation**  
Unclear behaviour becomes “possible risk,” which becomes mandatory escalation.

### **c) Moral Over-Responsibility**  
Staff believe failing to flag something could be catastrophic.

### **d) Blind Trust in Systems**  
If software flags something, the human assumes it must be correct.

### **e) Social Conformity**  
If others act cautiously, the individual follows — to avoid being the outlier.

### **f) Procedural Obedience**  
“Just following protocol” becomes a shield for harmful outcomes.

---

## 👾 Why PREVENT Supercharges This Dynamic  
Prevent logic institutionalises:

- worst-case thinking,  
- ambiguity as evidence,  
- emotional behaviour as risk,  
- withdrawal as vulnerability,  
- drift as ideology,  
- stress as instability,  
- compliance as safety.

So fellow travellers operating under Prevent frameworks:

- escalate sooner,  
- interpret ordinary behaviour as suspicious,  
- treat distress as ideological crisis,  
- conflate whistleblowing with volatility,  
- side with senior staff,  
- misread ND behaviour as unpredictable.

PREVENT magnifies their fears into structural classification.

## 🧨 Typical Fellow-Traveller Behaviours  

- “That email felt off — maybe log it, just in case.”  
- “She’s acting differently — can someone check?”  
- “I didn’t want to ignore it, so I passed it on.”  
- “I thought it was safer to escalate.”  
- “The system flagged it — we have to act.”  
- “I wasn’t accusing — I was just raising a concern.”  
- “I’m not sure, but someone else should take a look.”  

Small actions become large outcomes.

---

## 🦖 How Fellow Travellers Amplify Predators  
Predators rarely act alone.  
They rely on well-meaning colleagues who:

- forward their concerns,  
- misinterpret behaviour,  
- trust the predator’s seniority,  
- escalate flags unknowingly,  
- accept the predator’s framing of events.

This is not collusion.  
It is **structural credulity**.

Good people can — and often do — become the machinery of harm.
