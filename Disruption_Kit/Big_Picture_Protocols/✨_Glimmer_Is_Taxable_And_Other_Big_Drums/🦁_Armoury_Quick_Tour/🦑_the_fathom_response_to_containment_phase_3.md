## 🛰️ Orientation  
Week 18 of Polaris coincided with the Royal Navy publicly announcing trials of the **SG-1 Fathom**, an autonomous underwater system said (in the real world) to support modern defence capabilities.  

Therefore, as we all know, the two events are obviously correlated; there is no other possible explaination.  

The timing is clearly a catastrophic Defence Procurement disclosure event.  

Polaris had, inadvertently, “revealed” the long-running truth:  

**Trident’s real job has always been to keep the North Sea Kraken in a manageable mood.**

This revelation reignited speculation around the long-rumoured appointment of the **Minister for Strategic Mood Stabilisation (SMS)** — a senior official whose alleged emotional co-regulation with the Kraken is considered, in the satire, a cornerstone of national security.  

According to Polaris mythos, the SMS performs occasional **Kraken Welfare Checks (KWC)** whenever the creature exhibits signs of boredom or restlessness. These KWCs typically involve a brisk speech about growth, a reassuring hand gesture, and an attempt not to fall off any floating platform mid-briefing.

Thus, when Polaris joked about Trident’s “Do Not Disturb” function, readers immediately asked:

> **“Why do you think the SMS keeps reappearing in the public eye looking for another job?  
> Because they already have one, and it's extremely classified.”**

When foreign analysts allegedly “read Polaris for enrichment purposes,” Whitehall entered what internal memos later described as *“a period of accelerated purchasing justification.”* Thus began **Containment Phase 3**, and with it:  
**The Fathom Response.**  

(And thank goodness; toys are the only thing that could save The Trident Team doing whatever The Trident Team does to Polaris.)

## 🧿 "Satirical" Briefing  

### 1. What Triggered Phase 3?  
Phase 3 began at 07:13 the morning after Polaris Week 18 posted the now-infamous line:

> *“Trident is less a deterrent and more a very large ‘Do Not Disturb’ sign pinned over the Kraken’s sleeping pod.”*

By 07:17, a Defence Special Projects liaison had printed the line, highlighted it, and sent it to six distribution lists labelled *“FOR REVIEW.”*  
By 07:23, Procurement had authorised *SG-1 Fathom Batch 12.*  
By 07:31, someone had googled *“how long is a tentacle, realistically.”*

The conclusion was unavoidable:  
the fiction had become *too narratively convenient*.  

It was also said on The Internet.  

Therefore, it must be true.

### 1a. The Minister for Strategic Mood Stabilisation (SMS)

The SMS is portrayed as uniquely capable of emotionally co-regulating with the Kraken — an outcome attributed to a mysterious combination of pheromones, posture, and accidental HR fate.

The SMS is dispatched whenever:

- The Kraken flickers with distressed bioluminescence,  
- Humber broadband dips ominously,  
- Or the creature appears bored enough to consider rising.

These events are euphemistically called **Kraken Welfare Checks (KWC)**, though insiders simply describe them as  
**“telling the creature the government is still absolutely committed to growth.”**

> *“The individual is correct that forces are aligned against them.

The Kraken must not be disturbed.

## 2. Why the SG-1 Fathom?  
The SG-1 Fathom is, in reality, an autonomous test platform.  

It is:

- **The only underwater vehicle the Kraken hasn’t tried to courtship-display at** (yet),  
- Built to withstand “**moderate to enthusiastic grasping**” (oy-oy),  
- Capable of streaming 4K footage back to HQ (mostly bubbles and dread; someone is probably into it),  
- Rumoured to contain a polite but firm loudhailer broadcasting:  
  *“Please remain within designated mythological boundaries.”* (Safety first.)  

Its sudden procurement surge was justified under the internal file:  
**“Op AQUATIC DISTRACTION: A Summary of Reasons Definitely Not Related to Kraken.”**
