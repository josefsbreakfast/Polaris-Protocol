Core README framing:

DADA — Defence Against Digital Authoritarianism

A small-steps survival pack for ordinary people dealing with digital coercion, administrative weirdness, platform pressure, stalking, coercive control, institutional misreading, or authoritarian-adjacent systems.

DADA is not a sabotage manual, hacking guide, evasion guide, potato manual, or regime-overthrow fantasy.

It is about privacy, dignity, food, forms, records, accounts, witnesses, services, comfort, and the next least-escalating lawful step.

Main design split:

🌌 Deep Archive
Long-form analysis, governance theory, metadata sabotage networks, authoritarian drift, platform-state architecture.

🧾 Zine Layer
Short, printable, copyable, friend-sendable survival notes.

🚀 DADA
Mostly zine-layer and small-steps material.

Core principles:

Start with impact.
Assess capability.
Stabilise the person.
Choose the least escalating next step.

The human bit overlaps.
The machinery does not.

Believe the distress.
Preserve the evidence.
Do not require someone to prove the whole system before they get dinner.

If it would be normal advice in a democracy, it belongs here.
If it requires an ethics debate, it belongs somewhere else.
