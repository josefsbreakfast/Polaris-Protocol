## 🛰️ Orientation  

**Diagnostic Tools** provides operational frameworks for identifying, mapping, and analysing containment systems across:
- institutions,
- organisations,
- governance structures,
- communities,
- platforms,
- relationships,
- and emotional environments.

Where other areas of **🌸 Containment Studies** focus on:
- conceptual analysis,
- symbolic structures,
- governance dynamics,
- and systemic reflexes,

this cluster focuses on:
> practical diagnostic application.

The tools within this cluster are designed to help examine:
- how containment functions,
- where pressure accumulates,
- how rigidity emerges,
- who absorbs instability,
- and when stabilisation systems drift from care into coercion.

These tools are intended as:
- analytical frameworks,
- interpretive heuristics,
- and reflective mapping structures,

rather than:
- absolute classification systems,
- predictive certainties,
- or moral absolutes.

---

# 🩺 Core Premise  

Containment systems are often difficult to identify clearly because:
- many forms appear normal,
- stabilisation can feel protective,
- coercion frequently overlaps with care,
- and containment often emerges gradually rather than dramatically.

As a result:
> systems may become rigid long before they are recognised as harmful.

Diagnostic frameworks help make visible:
- hidden pressure distribution,
- institutional reflexes,
- stabilisation burdens,
- symbolic legitimisation,
- and patterns of adaptive or coercive governance.

This cluster therefore asks:
> what does containment look like operationally,
> and how can it be mapped without collapsing complexity into simplistic binaries?

## 🌸 Diagnostic Philosophy  

A recurring principle throughout this cluster is:
> diagnostic tools are interpretive aids, not objective machines.

All containment analysis involves:
- perspective,
- framing,
- value assumptions,
- historical context,
- and incomplete information.

This cluster therefore avoids:
- rigid moral absolutism,
- totalising classification,
- and simplistic binaries such as:
  - open vs closed,
  - safe vs unsafe,
  - coercive vs free.

Instead, the tools are designed to support:
- pattern recognition,
- systems reflection,
- governance analysis,
- and adaptive thinking under complexity.

---

## 🌉 Containment And Legibility  

One reason containment systems persist is because:
> stabilisation structures often become culturally invisible once normalised.

This includes:
- emotional labour becoming expected,
- surveillance becoming infrastructural,
- emergency logic becoming routine,
- symbolic rituals replacing scrutiny,
- and rigidity becoming mistaken for competence.

Diagnostic tools therefore function partly as:
> legibility mechanisms.

They help reveal:
- hidden assumptions,
- pressure transfer,
- burden asymmetry,
- and stabilisation reflexes that may otherwise remain unnamed.

At the same time, this cluster recognises:
> every diagnostic framework can itself become coercive if treated as absolute truth.

The purpose is therefore:
- illumination,
- not ideological purification.
